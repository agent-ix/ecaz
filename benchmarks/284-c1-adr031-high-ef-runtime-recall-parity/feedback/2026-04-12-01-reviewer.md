# Review: Packet 284 — C1 ADR-031 Higher-EF Runtime Recall Parity

Reviewer: agent-ix (via code-review)
Date: 2026-04-12

## Assessment: Useful read — correctly scoped conclusion despite ambiguous result

### What landed

Ran the external summary harness at `ef_search=128` and `ef_search=200` on the
same `m=8` 50K index, bounded 200-query table.

### On the results

```
ef_search=128: graph_below_exact_queries=1, worst_exact_gap=1
ef_search=200: graph_below_exact_queries=2, worst_exact_gap=1
```

Small divergence (1-2 queries, worst gap of 1 position), but the graph recall
is actually higher than the exact quantized recall versus fp32 truth. This is
not a regression — it's the normal HNSW behavior where higher ef_search
explores more graph paths and occasionally discovers results the exhaustive
quantized scan misses.

### On the interpretation

The packet correctly identifies the ambiguity: this could be new ADR-031
behavior or preexisting HNSW behavior. It correctly does **not** over-interpret
the result and instead flags the right follow-on (A/B comparison with ADR-031
enabled vs disabled, or a full gate rerun).

### One structural observation

The fact that `graph_recall_at_10 > exact_quantized_recall_at_10` at higher
ef_search is interesting. It means the graph path is finding neighbors that
score better under the quantizer than what the exhaustive quantized scan returns.
This can happen when HNSW explores graph neighborhoods that happen to contain
vectors whose quantized representations are closer to the query than what a
linear scan of all quantized vectors would find — essentially, the graph
topology provides a beneficial sampling bias at higher ef_search.

This is not specific to ADR-031 and would be worth confirming on the
pre-ADR-031 baseline if the question comes up again.

### Verdict

Clean scoping. The packet does not over-claim parity and does not panic over a
tiny divergence. The correct call is that ADR-031 validation is sufficient at
the latency target seam, and the higher-ef question is parked until it actually
blocks something.
