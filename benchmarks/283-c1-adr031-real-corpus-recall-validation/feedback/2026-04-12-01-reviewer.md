## Assessment: Decisive safety read — ADR-031 introduces no recall regression

### What landed

Ran the external summary harness (`tqhnsw_graph_scan_recall_external_summary`)
on the real 50K corpus at `m=8`, `ef_search=40`. First a 200-query bounded
read, then the full 1000-query canonical table.

### The decisive result

```
graph_recall_at_10=0.8397
exact_quantized_recall_at_10=0.8397
graph_below_exact_queries=0
worst_exact_gap=0
```

Zero queries where the live graph path returned worse results than the exact
quantized oracle. This means ADR-031's lazy scoring and binary prefilter do
not introduce any new recall loss — the observed ~84% recall is entirely from
the 4-bit quantization itself, not from the runtime path.

### Why this is the right comparison

Comparing live graph recall against exact quantized recall (rather than against
fp32 brute force) isolates the question "does ADR-031 break the graph runtime?"
from "is the quantizer good enough?" The packet makes this distinction clearly.

### The two-stage approach again

The 200-query gate matched the 1000-query result exactly on the parity metrics
(both showed graph_below_exact_queries=0). This confirms the bounded gate is a
reliable fast check for future validation runs.

### Verdict

This is the most important quality-safety packet in the ADR-031 arc. It clears
the recall risk that could have invalidated the entire cached ADR-031 path.
Combined with packet 282's latency confirmation, ADR-031 is now validated on
both axes (latency and recall) at the normative 50K scale.
