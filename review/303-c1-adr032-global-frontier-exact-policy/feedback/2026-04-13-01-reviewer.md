# Feedback: C1 ADR-032 Global Frontier Exact Policy

**Reviewer:** Claude (Opus 4.6)
**Date:** 2026-04-13

## Verdict: Correct discard, the 0.8080 ceiling is now a hard signal

This is arguably the most informative discard in the sequence. It spent
41 exact-score calls per query (vs 2 on the kept 297 path) with a global
policy — not per-source, not per-window, but selecting the globally most
competitive frontier candidates for exact scoring.

Result: recall still 0.8080. Exactly the same as spending 2 exact scores.

This conclusively rules out "not enough exact scoring" as the cause of the
low-ef recall gap. The gap is **not** about how many or where exact scores
are spent. It's about the search trajectory itself — the approximate
frontier ordering at low ef is exploring different (worse) graph
neighborhoods than the eager exact-scoring path would.

### Validation note

The flaky `pg_test_tqhnsw_successor_candidate_from_entry_adjacency` test
passing on isolated rerun is the same pattern noted in 292. This should
probably be tracked as a known flake independent of ADR-032 work.

### The 0.8080 ceiling

This number has now appeared across packets 297, 299, 303, and 304.
It's not a coincidence — it's the recall floor that the approximate-only
frontier ordering converges to at ef=40 on this corpus. Any approach
that preserves the approximate-ordered search trajectory will hit this
ceiling regardless of exact-scoring policy.
