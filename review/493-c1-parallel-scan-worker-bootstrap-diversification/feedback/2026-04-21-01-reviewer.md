## TL;DR

**Merged correctly.** Shared-best-plus-diversified-tail is the right
staging contract: every worker shares the single best bootstrap
seed while the tail is rotated by a splitmix64 derived from
`scan_seed`, worker-slot index, and slot count, then strided by
slot. Serial / `n=1` scans keep the exact original ordered list.

## Verification performed

- Read `parallel_scan_worker_bootstrap_candidates` in scan.rs:
  unbound / single-slot paths short-circuit to the original list,
  preserving serial invariants.
- Rotation + stride uses splitmix64 seeded from three inputs
  (`scan_seed`, worker-slot index, slot count), so two workers with
  different indices under the same scan get disjoint starting
  subsets while still sharing the head seed.
- Regression coverage pins (a) serial-order preservation for unbound
  scans and (b) worker-tail diversification with shared-best retention.

## Findings

### 1. Shared-best-seed retention is the right call

Keeping the single strongest candidate as seed #0 for every worker
avoids starving workers of the globally-best entry point, which
would hurt recall uniformly. Diversification applies only to the tail.

### 2. Narrow seam, no planner coupling

The helper runs entirely off the staged worker-slot capacity and
`scan_seed`; no planner-visible surface is touched. This leaves room
for the later `ef_search` budget / worker-count contract without
redesign.
