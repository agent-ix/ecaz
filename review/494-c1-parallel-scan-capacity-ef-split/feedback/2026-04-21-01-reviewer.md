# Feedback: Parallel Scan Capacity-Based ef_search Split

**Reviewer:** Claude (Opus 4.7)
**Date:** 2026-04-21
**Head reviewed:** `f41409c` (landed)

## TL;DR

**Merged correctly.** `ec_hnsw.parallel_ef_overlap` (default `0.1`,
range `[0.0, 0.5]`) plus `resolve_parallel_scan_ef_search` implement
the staged `ceil(ef/worker_slots) + overlap` split. Serial
`effective_ef_search` is preserved when worker-slot capacity is 0 or
1, so serial and `n=1` invariants are unchanged.

## Verification performed

- GUC bounds match ADR-style recommendations: non-negative floor,
  `0.5` cap prevents the overlap multiplier from turning the split
  into "every worker does the full serial budget."
- Bootstrap sizing now routes through `resolve_bootstrap_frontier_limit`
  in `ec_hnsw_amrescan`, not a direct `effective_ef_search` copy.
- Unit coverage pins serial fallback, split-plus-overlap math, and
  scan-side frontier-limit resolution.

## Findings

### 1. Capacity, not live worker count — correctly flagged

The helper uses `EcParallelScanState` worker-slot capacity because
executor-visible worker count isn't plumbed yet. The packet names
this explicitly as a staging stand-in. When the real worker-count
wiring lands, this call site becomes behavioral and must be revisited.

### 2. Overlap cap is the right ceiling

`[0.0, 0.5]` is tight enough to prevent the overlap from eating the
entire split benefit while still giving recall headroom when workers
overlap on a small tail. Narrow GUC surface.
