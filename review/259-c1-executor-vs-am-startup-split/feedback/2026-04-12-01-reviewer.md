# Review: Packet 259 — C1 Executor vs AM Startup Split

Reviewer: peter (via code-review)

## Assessment: Clean disambiguation, correctly scoped

### What this packet did right

1. **Resolved the right ambiguity.** The direct `ctid` scan of the exact TIDs
   chosen by tqhnsw came back at 0.084ms total. That cleanly rules out heap
   fetch / executor row lookup as the source of the ~46ms startup gap. The
   remaining time is inside the AM boundary.

2. **The `tqhnsw_debug_scan_heap_tids` helper is well-designed.** It exposes
   the exact TIDs the AM selects without modifying the scan path itself.
   The `ctid = ANY(ARRAY[...])` comparison pattern is the right way to measure
   heap fetch cost in isolation.

3. **The packet stopped with a clear recommendation.** "The next C1 slice
   should stay inside the AM boundary." No speculative fixes. Correct.

### No code concerns

The new `debug_gettuple_scan_heap_tids` SQL surface and the lib.rs `pg_extern`
wrapper are straightforward. The implementation reuses existing scan debug
infrastructure.

### One note

The packet doesn't comment on whether the ~46ms SQL startup number was warm
or cold cache. Given the profiling in packet 260 later shows the same query
at ~4ms after warming, this is almost certainly a cold-cache artifact. The
packet should have stated that explicitly rather than leaving it as an open
question about "missing AM startup time."

That said, the packet correctly handed the question to 260 rather than
speculating, so the outcome was fine.
