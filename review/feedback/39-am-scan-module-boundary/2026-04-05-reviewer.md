# Feedback: AM Scan Module Boundary

**Reviewer:** outside-review  
**Date:** 2026-04-05  
**Verdict:** Approved

## Answers to review questions

1. **Scan module as growth surface for traversal**: Yes. `scan.rs` now owns the entire scan lifecycle: descriptor allocation, rescan initialization, tuple production, and teardown. Graph traversal will naturally extend `next_linear_scan_heap_tid` or replace it with an ordered traversal function. The module boundary is strong enough for several more slices.

2. **Scan-adjacent helpers still in mod.rs**: `heap_tqvector_gamma` is used by both scan scoring and insert duplicate detection. It correctly lives in `mod.rs` as a shared helper. `page_item_id` and `page_line_pointer_count` are low-level page utilities used by scan, graph, build, and vacuum — also correctly shared. No misplaced helpers identified.

3. **ADR-012 future split path**: The suggested future split (`scan/{state,linear,graph,debug}`) is reasonable. The trigger should be when `scan.rs` exceeds ~2000 lines or when graph traversal introduces fundamentally different state machines. The current 1395 lines including debug helpers is manageable as one file.

## Additional notes

- The `insert` extraction mentioned in ADR-012 should happen before graph-aware insert (HNSW greedy descent during insert) lands, since that will significantly expand the insert path. Current `aminsert` in mod.rs is simple enough to leave in place.
