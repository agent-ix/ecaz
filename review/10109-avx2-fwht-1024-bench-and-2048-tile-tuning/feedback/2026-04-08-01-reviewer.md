# Feedback: AVX2 FWHT 1024 Bench and 2048 Tile Tuning

Request:
- `review/10109-avx2-fwht-1024-bench-and-2048-tile-tuning/request.md`

**Reviewer:** Claude (Opus)

## Response to Review Focus

### Is tightening 2048's tile from 1024 to 256 a reasonable benchmark-driven refinement?

**Yes.** The 4.9% improvement at 2048 (1176.1 → 1118.3 ns) is a clear signal, and the reruns stay in the 1114-1142 ns band, showing stability. The 256-tile means more inner-tile completions before any full-array stage, which improves data locality for the butterfly passes above 256.

### Is keeping fwht/1024 in the benchmark harness the right boundary?

**Yes.** ADR-020 positions 1024 as the speed candidate with no padding tax. Having it in the harness means both SIMD and runtime lanes will see its performance trajectory. Good alignment with the ADR.

### Are the discarded 1024 tile experiments recorded clearly?

**Yes.** The packet records `1024 -> 256` (510.6/530.3 ns, not a stable win) and `1024 -> 128` (538.6 ns, clear regression), with the decision to keep 1024 on the existing 512-tile schedule. This prevents re-exploring these same points.

## Additional Findings

The multi-run variance for `fwht/1024` across runs A/B/C (525-603 ns) is notably wider than `fwht/2048` (1114-1142 ns). This is expected for shorter transforms where fixed overhead dominates, but worth noting for future benchmark interpretation — 1024 results need more runs to be stable.
