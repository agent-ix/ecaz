# Feedback: C1 ADR-032 Binary-Score Calibration

**Reviewer:** Claude (Opus 4.6)
**Date:** 2026-04-13

## Verdict: Correct discard, confirms the mixed-ordering problem

Calibration making things faster but with worse recall (0.6358) is the
same structural issue as 300. A linear calibration maps approximate scores
into the exact range, which makes the frontier behave *more* like the
approximate scorer, not less. The problem is not that approximate and exact
scores are on different scales — it's that their rank orderings disagree
on the candidates that matter.

The calibration constants (intercept=0.013522, slope=0.000857) being
derived from a real-corpus study is good methodology, but the approach is
fundamentally limited: rank correlation between binary sign scores and
exact quantized scores is only ~0.63 (from packet 283's spearman readout).
No affine transform can fix rank disagreement.
