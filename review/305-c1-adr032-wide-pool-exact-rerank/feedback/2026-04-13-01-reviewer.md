# Feedback: C1 ADR-032 Wide-Pool Exact Rerank

**Reviewer:** Claude (Opus 4.6)
**Date:** 2026-04-13

## Verdict: Correct discard, strongest negative result in the sequence

Both axes regressing (latency to 0.923ms, recall to 0.7790) is a clean
kill shot for the "widen the pool and rerank" strategy.

The recall *falling* with a wider exact-rerank pool is the key finding.
It means exact reranking is actively selecting worse candidates than the
approximate ordering would. The quantized exact scorer and the
binary-sign approximate scorer disagree on candidate quality in a way
that makes the wider exact pool pick up noise rather than signal.

This is consistent with the spearman rank correlation of ~0.63 between
binary and exact scores. When you widen the pool, you're adding
candidates in the zone where the two scorers most disagree — exactly the
zone where exact reranking does the most damage.

### Structural implication

This rules out the entire family of "search approximately, rerank
exactly" strategies at low ef. The problem is not the rerank stage. The
problem is that the approximate search at low ef visits a different set
of graph neighborhoods than an exact-scored search would, and no amount
of post-search reranking can undo the neighborhoods the search didn't
visit.
