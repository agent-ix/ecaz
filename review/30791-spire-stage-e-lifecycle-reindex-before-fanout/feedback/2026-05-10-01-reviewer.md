## Answers to the packet's review questions

- **Is including `pg_relation_filenode(index_oid)` in the
  endpoint fingerprint the right generation boundary for
  detecting remote REINDEX?** Yes. The filenode is the
  authoritative PostgreSQL identifier for the physical
  storage of an index; REINDEX CONCURRENTLY changes it,
  and a descriptor that hasn't observed the new filenode
  is exactly the kind of stale binding the identity
  contract should catch. The alternative (adding a
  separate `generation_identity` field) would create two
  parallel identity tokens that could drift; folding
  filenode into the existing fingerprint keeps one
  source of truth.
- **Does the lifecycle matrix now separate executor
  status `remote_candidate_receive_failed` from detection
  category `endpoint_identity_mismatch`?** Yes — and
  this is the right separation. The *status* says where
  in the executor state machine the failure was
  observed; the *category* says what specifically was
  wrong. Operators see both: "the executor stopped at
  candidate receive" (status) and "the endpoint identity
  disagreed with what we expected" (category). Same
  shape as packet 30782's fingerprint-mismatch fault
  row, which also surfaces `endpoint_identity_mismatch`
  through `remote_candidate_receive_failed`.
- **Does the degraded pass correctly rewrite the remote
  reindexed endpoint to degraded mode before expecting a
  degraded skip?** Yes — verified by the artifact log
  showing the degraded summary's
  `first_degraded_skip_category=endpoint_identity_mismatch`
  with the ready remote completing.
