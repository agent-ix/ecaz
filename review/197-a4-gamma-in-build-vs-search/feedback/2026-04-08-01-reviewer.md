## Verdict: Gamma Is Inert on This Corpus — Not a Recall Driver

### Ablation evidence (from review 201)

| Variant | Recall@10 |
|---------|-----------|
| exact (with gamma) | 49.5% |
| gamma_zero | 49.0% |

Gamma contributes 0.5% recall. The hypothesis that gamma variance causes traversal to "walk away from
the true nearest neighbors" is not supported by the data.

### Why gamma is inert

Looking at `score_ip_from_split_parts` in `prod.rs:174-199`:

```rust
mse_sum + gamma * prepared.qjl_scale * qjl_sum
```

Where `qjl_scale = (PI / 2.0).sqrt() / original_dim as f32`.

For d=1536: `qjl_scale ≈ 1.254 / 1536 ≈ 0.000816`.

The QJL sum is over 1536 random ±1 signs times query projections — by CLT, this concentrates around 0
with moderate variance. Multiplied by gamma (a per-element scalar, typically small) and the tiny
qjl_scale, the gamma*QJL term is negligible relative to the MSE LUT sum.

This is consistent with the 0.5% ablation result. The QJL correction was designed as a lightweight
residual, and on this corpus it adds almost nothing to ranking precision.

### What about gamma on different data?

On structured data where gamma might carry more per-element information (e.g., varying norms after
rotation), this term could matter more. Worth re-checking if the quantizer is tested on real
embeddings. But for the current A4 investigation, gamma is not the issue.
