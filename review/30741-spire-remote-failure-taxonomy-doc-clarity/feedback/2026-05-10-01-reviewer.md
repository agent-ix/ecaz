## P3 — informational

1. **`is_closed()` precedence note.** The classifier checks
   `is_closed()` first, then `as_db_error()`. The comments could
   note that this ordering is deliberate — a backend that closes
   the connection mid-query may also have a stale db_error in the
   tokio-postgres state from earlier in the session, and we want
   the closure to take precedence. Worth one short sentence near
   the `is_closed()` branch. Optional.

2. **SQLSTATE `57P01`/`57P02`/`57P03` test coverage.** The
   classifier now branches on these in addition to closed
   connections, but I don't see a dedicated fixture that tickles
   them. They're rare in practice (admin shutdown, crash, cannot
   connect now), but a focused unit test that constructs a fake
   `db_error` with each SQLSTATE would lock the branch behavior
   without needing a real PostgreSQL crash. Optional; the
   existing closed-connection fixture covers the most common
   case.
