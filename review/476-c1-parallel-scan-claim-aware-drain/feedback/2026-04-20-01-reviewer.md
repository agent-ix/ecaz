## Findings

### 1. Claim-liveness check order matters

**Severity:** Informational (positive)
**File:** `src/am/common/parallel.rs:700–713`

`coordinator_result_slot_worker_claim_is_live` loads `observed_rescan_epoch`
then `flags`, both `Acquire`. If a release is in progress (flag
CAS'd from CLAIMED → FREE), the acquire ordering ensures the caller
either sees the still-CLAIMED state (and treats the slot as live) or
sees FREE (and rejects it). The Release store on the CAS publishes
the slot-reset writes. Sound as a single-writer protocol; when
`amcanparallel` flips on, this remains correct for the release-side
but the claimant-side still needs the coordinator-rebuild serialization
flagged in 472.

### 2. Liveness cost is bounded

**Severity:** Informational
**File:** `src/am/common/parallel.rs:723–736, 1361–1387`

Every read/take now does up to two slot-liveness evaluations
(snapshot liveness + worker-claim liveness). That's four atomic loads
per liveness check, bounded and cache-friendly. No concern.

### 3. Refresh-before-exposing is the right contract

**Severity:** Informational (positive)

The question "is refresh-before-exposing the right seam for later
coordinator merge work?" — yes. This keeps the refresh logic in one
place and avoids teaching every consumer about claim-liveness. Keep
this pattern for the real top-K merge slice.
