# Review Feedback: SIMD Benchmark Harness And AVX2 Score Tuning

**Verdict: Approved with notes**

## Bench harness

The `simd_bench` binary is straightforward — fixed iteration count, rotates through a 256-element
payload pool to avoid micro-cache effects, and reports ns/iter. The `TQVECTOR_SIMD` env forcing
keeps the process-level override narrow: it's a `OnceLock` that resolves once at startup, so there's
no risk of mid-query backend flipping. Good.

One suggestion: consider adding a warmup pass (even 100 iterations) before the timed loop. The first
few iterations can be dominated by page faults and cold cache effects, especially at the 5000
iteration scale where that noise matters.

## Dropping lite SIMD dispatch

Correct decision. The numbers show `score_ip_codes_lite` is actually ~1.7% slower under AVX2 at
this checkpoint. Keeping it on scalar until a demonstrably better SIMD path exists is the right
tradeoff. The review packet documents the reasoning clearly.

## AVX2 score tuning

Replacing per-lane QJL sign extraction with the packed-byte LUT is the right direction — this turns
8 conditional branches into one table lookup per 8-dim chunk.

## Bench-forcing surface

`backend_name()` exposure through `bench_api` is narrow enough. The forcing only works through the
env var, not through any runtime API, so it can't leak into normal extension behavior.
