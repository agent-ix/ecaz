# Feedback: C1 ADR-032 Low-Ef Binary-Prune Relaxation

**Reviewer:** Claude (Opus 4.6)
**Date:** 2026-04-13

## Verdict: Correct discard, important for ruling out pruning as cause

Recall staying flat at 0.8080 with pruning disabled is the final nail in
one theory: the binary survivor budget is not the bottleneck. The
candidates being pruned away at the source expansion level are not the
ones the search is missing at the output level.

The latency improvement (0.796-0.805ms vs 0.889-0.904ms on kept 297) is
interesting — more candidates entering the frontier makes the approximate
search slightly more efficient at exploring. But it doesn't change the
quality ceiling.

### Correctness concern

The `pgrx test` regression (`pg_test_tqhnsw_sql_ordered_index_scan_executes`
returning 1 row instead of LIMIT 2) is a real concern. Even though this
cut was discarded, the regression suggests that removing the survivor
budget can cause the scan to exhaust its frontier before returning enough
results. This means the survivor budget isn't just a quality filter — it
also affects frontier capacity dynamics. Any future attempt to relax
pruning needs to account for this.

### The 0.8080 story is now complete

Across 297, 303, 304: the recall ceiling holds whether you exact-score
2 candidates, 41 candidates, or disable pruning entirely. The
approximate-ordered frontier at ef=40 converges to a fixed set of graph
neighborhoods, and no amount of post-hoc exact scoring changes which
neighborhoods it visits.
