  linear scan with `compare_exchange(FREE, CLAIMED, AcqRel, Acquire)`.
  Ordering is correct — AcqRel on success publishes the reset, Acquire

- The pre-CAS epoch check (`parallel.rs:1063–1066`) is the right gate

## Findings

### 1. Claim path has no backoff on CAS contention

**Severity:** Informational (not a bug at current scale)
**File:** `src/am/common/parallel.rs:1060–1087`

Claim walks slots in index order; on CAS failure it silently advances.
With `max_parallel_workers_per_gather + 1` slots (typically ≤ 9), this
is fine. Once `amcanparallel` flips on and real workers start, a small
number of workers racing to claim slot 0 together will each miss once
before finding their own slot. The retry cost is negligible today, but
worth noting: if future parallelism scales to larger worker counts, a
starting-slot hash (e.g. `ParallelWorkerNumber % worker_slot_count`)
gives O(1) expected claim time.

### 2. Rescan epoch guard is sufficient for stale cleanup

**Severity:** Informational (positive)

The question "is the rescan-epoch guard sufficient for stale
worker-slot cleanup without a more complex generation protocol?" — yes.
`rescan_epoch` is written once by the leader inside `amparallelrescan`
before worker attachments (`parallel.rs:1458`), and workers attach
through `validate_parallel_scan_state` which copies the leader's
epoch into the attachment. Any stale-epoch release becomes an idempotent
no-op. No generation counter is needed unless/until rescans can race
with in-flight workers — which `amcanparallel = false` prevents.
