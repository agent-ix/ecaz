## Key signals from the baseline

1. **Saturation confirmed.** Recall@10 reaches 1.000 from
   nprobe=16 upward in every rerank mode. nprobe=8 gives
   recall@10 = 0.9950 (one miss in 100 queries). real10k cannot
   demonstrate anisotropic or IMI quality upside on its own.
2. **Adaptive `nprobe` has a real target.** At rerank_width=25,
   nprobe=8 is 73ms p50 vs nprobe=32 at 188ms p50 — 2.6× latency
   delta for one extra recall@10 miss. Adaptive policy that drops
   to nprobe=8 on easy queries while retaining nprobe=32 (or
   higher) on hard queries is well-motivated by this data.
3. **Rerank=0 is the slow path** (576ms p50 at nprobe=8 vs 73ms
   at rerank=25), but it does not move recall — the SPIRE candidate
   set itself already contains the true top-10 at the rates that
   define the ceiling. This is fixture-side saturation, not a
   rerank correctness issue.
4. **rerank=25 vs rerank=50 are nearly identical** in both
   recall and latency. Operators tuning only for recall on this
   fixture have no reason to go above rerank=25; the bounded
   accumulator from Phase 10.1 does its job.
