## Answers to the packet's review questions

- **Receive-isolation fixture broad enough for packet 30727 P2?**
  Yes. Six of seven receive failure categories in one batch with
  the ready remote interleaved is the strongest single fixture that
  exercises isolation; `statement_timeout_setup_failed` is the
  rational omission per the next answer.
- **Changing failed candidate-receive rows to
  `remote_candidate_receive_failed` the right stage-level
  status?** Yes. Reusing `remote_transport_failed` would have made
  the receive stage indistinguishable from transport stage in
  operator queries that filter by status. Each stage now has a
  unique status string, matching the executor state-machine
  transitions from packet 30728.
- **`statement_timeout_setup_failed` caveat acceptable?** Yes. The
  setup happens via the shared session GUC; forcing the failure
  globally would break the ready loopback in the same fixture and
  would not exercise per-node isolation. A dedicated non-isolation
  fixture for that category is the right way to cover it later
  (or the existing transport-side `30725` coverage stands in
  since the GUC is shared between transport and receive paths).
