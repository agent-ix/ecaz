# Feedback: C1 ADR-032 Exact-On-Head Frontier Promotion

**Reviewer:** Claude (Opus 4.6)
**Date:** 2026-04-13

## Verdict: Kept, breakthrough result, recall gap now quantified

### Latency result is decisive

| ef_search | ADR-031 T1 mean | ADR-032 mean | speedup |
|-----------|----------------|--------------|---------|
| 40        | ~1.51ms        | ~0.90ms      | ~1.68x  |
| 128       | ~3.41ms        | ~1.66ms      | ~2.05x  |
| 200       | ~4.77ms        | ~2.38ms      | ~2.00x  |

This is a consistent ~2x improvement at higher ef. Real.

### Recall gap is now quantified against ADR-031 T1 baseline

The ADR-031 T1 baseline at `ef=40` on the same 1000-query corpus was:

- Packet 287: `graph_recall_at_10 = 0.8428`
- Packet 283: `graph_recall_at_10 = 0.8397`

ADR-032 at `ef=40`: `graph_recall_at_10 = 0.8080`.

That is a **3.2-3.5 percentage point recall regression** at ef=40. This is
not parity with the quantized floor — ADR-031 T1 was above it, and ADR-032
dropped to it.

At `ef=128`, ADR-032 recall of `0.8861` exceeds the ADR-031 T1 `ef=40`
recall of `0.8428` while being faster than ADR-031 T1 at `ef=40`. That
means the operating frontier *is* better — you can get higher recall at
lower latency by running ADR-032 at a higher ef.

### The important framing question

The recall regression at ef=40 is real, but the question is whether it
matters operationally. If the deployment target is "best recall at a fixed
latency budget," then ADR-032 at ef=128 (mean 1.66ms, recall 0.8861)
strictly dominates ADR-031 T1 at ef=40 (mean 1.51ms, recall 0.8428).

The regression only matters if the deployment target is "best recall at
ef=40 specifically" — i.e., if ef_search is user-controlled and users
expect recall to not regress at the same ef value.

### Code note

The `exact_quantized_recall_at_10` comparator routing through the live
index is correctly flagged as unreliable. Good that the packet explicitly
calls this out.
