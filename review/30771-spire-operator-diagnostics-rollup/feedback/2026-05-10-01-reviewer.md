## Answers to the packet's review questions

- **Right one-row operator view for readiness / served
  epoch / fanout / candidate batches / heap resolution /
  merge / result source / AM delivery blocker state?** Yes.
  This is the surface an operator needs to answer "what's
  happening to my distributed scan right now" with one
  query. The 12+ existing surfaces it composes are valuable
  for deep diagnostics; this rollup is the dashboard view.
- **Excluding the local node from ready/blocked remote-node
  counts correct?** Yes. An operator inspecting the
  diagnostic wants to know "how many *remote* nodes can I
  serve from?" — folding the local node into either count
  conflates "can I scan locally?" (always yes if the index
  exists) with "can I fan out remotely?" Splitting them is
  the right ergonomic choice.
- **Clear that this is a diagnostic rollup over existing
  production surfaces, not the multi-instance fault fixture
  itself?** Yes. The function name says "operator
  diagnostics", not "fault fixture", and the task file
  language is consistent. No risk of operators mistaking
  the diagnostic for the Stage E fixture.
- **Phase 11 task update correctly scoped with replica-
  manifest freshness fixture evidence still open?** Yes.
  Replica-manifest freshness is one of the Stage E
  capabilities (along with epoch-skew, version-skew, etc.)
  and needs a multi-coordinator fixture to drive
  publication/observation. Naming it as still-open
  separates the *diagnostic surface* (this packet) from the
  *evidence* (Stage E fixture work).
