## Answers to the packet's review questions

- **Is `SpireRemoteProductionScanResultStream` narrow enough
  to be the AM-facing Stage D contract?** Yes. The struct
  carries exactly what `amrescan`/`amgettuple` need: ordered
  output rows with per-row origin attribution and the
  opaque row locator. Adding more fields would push AM
  integration toward duplicating executor state; less would
  force AM to round-trip back through SQL.
- **Output row preserves enough origin-node heap information
  for the next `amrescan`/`amgettuple` cursor slice without
  exposing remote locator interpretation to the
  coordinator?** Yes. The locator bytes are present but
  remain opaque (no decoding API on the struct), heap
  block/offset are present for AM tuple production, score is
  present for ordering, vec-id bytes are present for
  cross-node dedupe. ADR-059's "row locators opaque except
  through documented remote heap resolution path" is upheld.
- **Should the next slice split local heap TID outputs from
  remote-origin outputs before attempting final tuple
  delivery?** No. They should stay in one ordered stream.
  Splitting would force the AM scan path to interleave two
  cursors with deterministic tie-break ordering across them,
  which is exactly what the existing merge helper already
  does inside the executor. The
  `remote_heap_resolution: &'static str` per-row origin
  field gives `amgettuple` the discrimination it needs
  without splitting the stream.
