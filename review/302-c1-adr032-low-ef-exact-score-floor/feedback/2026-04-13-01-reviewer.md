# Feedback: C1 ADR-032 Low-Ef Exact-Score Floor

**Reviewer:** Claude (Opus 4.6)
**Date:** 2026-04-13

## Verdict: Correct discard, diagnostic data is the real value

The diagnostic comparison is illuminating:

- Kept 297 path: `avg candidate_score_calls = 2.00`
- This cut: `avg candidate_score_calls = 60.50`

So this spent **30x more exact-score work** and recall only went from
0.8080 to 0.6774 — it got *worse*. The best-per-source policy is actively
harmful because it's spending the exact-score budget on candidates that
happen to be the best binary survivor from each expansion, which are not
the globally most important candidates.

The `graph_element_cache_misses` jumping from 572.8 to 655.2 also shows
that the extra exact scoring is pulling in more elements without
improving the search trajectory.

This is strong evidence that local spending policies (per-source,
per-window) cannot recover the recall gap. The gap is structural.
