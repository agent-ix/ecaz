## C1 — Composition logic is correct

`src/am/ec_spire/root/hierarchy_snapshots.rs` adds:

1. `remote_search_coordinator_local_candidates_for_result_summary` — builds a
   *local-PID-only* `SpirePublishedEpochSnapshot` from filtered placement and
   object manifests before opening object stores, so mixed local+remote plans
   never reach for non-local placement metadata. This is the right shape; it
   sidesteps the pre-existing local-summary path's broader gating and keeps
   result-summary local resolution self-contained.
2. `merge_remote_search_heap_candidates_for_result` — `HashMap<vec_id, …>`
   keeps the best row per vec-id under `remote_search_candidate_cmp`, then
   sorts the survivors by the same comparator and truncates to `top_k`. This
   matches `ec_spire_remote_search_merge_order_contract()`'s documented order
   (primary placements ahead of boundary replicas on score ties, etc.).
3. `remote_search_coordinator_result_summary_row` — gates remote-heap retrieval
   on `gate.status == SPIRE_REMOTE_EXECUTOR_REQUIRED` and remote-plan presence,
   then derives `result_source`, `final_heap_fetch_status`, and `next_blocker`
   from the merged row count plus the heap-lookup-owner mix. `degraded_ready`
   propagates when `gate.skipped_plan_count > 0` — the prior feedback's
   degradation invariant is preserved through composition.

Mixed-fixture coverage in `test_ec_spire_remote_search_libpq_executor_loopback_empty`
asserts `result_source = remote_heap_candidates`, `returned_candidate_count = 2`,
`decoded_local_locator_count = 1`, `final_heap_fetch_status = remote_ready`,
`status = ready` — i.e. one local + one remote heap row dedup-merge. Good.

`SPIRE_REMOTE_RESULT_SOURCE_REMOTE_HEAP_CANDIDATES` and
`SPIRE_REMOTE_FINAL_STATUS_REMOTE_READY` are added as constants and threaded
through `ec_spire_remote_search_coordinator_result_contract()` per the request.
