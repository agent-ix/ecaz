## Answers to the packet's review questions

- **Cleanup ownership now explicit enough for the upcoming
  materialized-row provider design?** Yes. The provider
  packet now has a clear constraint:
  - cleanup is *not* the AM scan's job (no
    `amrescan`/`amgettuple` writes);
  - cleanup runs through epoch maintenance / operator mirror
    lifecycle.
  The provider implementer can choose between
  VACUUM-style sweep, `ec_spire`-internal compaction
  triggered by epoch retire events, or operator-driven
  via `ecaz` maintenance commands — without re-litigating
  whether the AM cursor should own the lifecycle (the
  answer is unambiguously no).
- **ADR-059/ADR-064 boundary reads correctly: origin
  visibility first, same-relation coordinator heap
  materialization second?** Yes. The bidirectional cross-
  references make the serial composition explicit. ADR-059
  produces visible-then-blocked rows; ADR-064 turns visible
  rows into AM-deliverable TIDs in the scanned relation.
  Neither ADR can be read in isolation and reach a
  contradictory conclusion.
- **Clear that cleanup is outside the AM scan cursor
  path?** Yes. Both ADRs name the boundary, and ADR-064
  explicitly excludes scan-time heap writes. A future
  reader looking at scan code cannot mistakenly add
  cleanup logic without contradicting documented invariants.

## Status

Closed. Cleanup ownership is the last design question that
needed pinning before the materialized-row provider
implementation. With ADR-059, ADR-064, the mapping contract
from packet 30765, and the AM cursor wiring from packet
30762 all locked, the provider implementation has a fully
specified target.
