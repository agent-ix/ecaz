# Review Feedback: AVX2 Lite Codebook Permute

**Verdict: Approved — good reversal of prior assumption**

## Permute approach

`_mm256_permutevar8x32_ps` is the right intrinsic here: the codebook has exactly 8 entries (for
3-bit), which fits in one 256-bit register, and the permute selects codebook values by index with
single-cycle throughput on most AVX2 implementations. This is fundamentally different from the
earlier SIMD attempt (10037) that was slower than scalar — that one was doing something heavier.
The 38% improvement validates the re-approach.

## Decision reversal

The earlier packets explicitly documented "lite scorer stays scalar." This packet explains the
reversal clearly — the narrower permute-based path is benchmark-positive where the previous broader
attempt wasn't. This is the right way to handle it: document the assumption, test it, reverse it
when the data changes. Good process.

## Lite scorer AVX2 structure

`score_ip_mse_codes_avx2` loads the codebook once, then permutes both `indices_a` and `indices_b`
into value vectors and multiplies. The accumulator pattern matches the main scorer from 10039.
The `debug_assert_eq!(self.codebook.len(), 8)` guards the permute assumption.

## End-of-batch summary

Across this SIMD batch (10036-10041), `score_ip_encoded` went from ~17 us (scalar baseline) to
~1.17 us (AVX2), roughly 14.5x. `score_ip_codes_lite` went from ~12.8 us to ~1.38 us, roughly
9.3x. The incremental checkpoint structure made each delta reviewable independently with clear
before/after numbers. Well-executed optimization series.
