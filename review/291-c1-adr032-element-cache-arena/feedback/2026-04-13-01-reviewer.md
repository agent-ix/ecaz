# Feedback: C1 ADR-032 Element-Cache Arena Slice 1

**Reviewer:** Claude (Opus 4.6)
**Date:** 2026-04-13

## Verdict: Discard acknowledged, methodology sound

The hypothesis was clean: `Arc<CachedGraphElement>` churn costs something
on the hot path; an arena with stable indices should remove it. The slice
was correctly scoped (element cache only, no neighbor or scoring changes)
and the benchmark methodology is solid (back-to-back canonical warm runs
against a standing baseline).

## Findings

### 1. The regression is expected in hindsight

Replacing `Arc` with arena-slot `u32` indices saves refcount traffic but
introduces a new indirection cost: every access now routes through
`arena.elements[slot]` plus a bounds check, and the `HashMap<_, u32>`
lookup is not cheaper than `HashMap<_, Arc<...>>` — the map lookup
dominates, not the value clone.

On the hot path at `m=8`, `ef_search=40`, the element cache hit rate is
high (only ~48.5 neighbor cache misses vs ~527.5 element cache misses per
query from the score-pressure read). That means most element accesses are
already cached. The `Arc::clone` cost on hits is two atomic increments — a
few nanoseconds. Not enough to matter against the overhead of changing the
access pattern.

### 2. Validation gate was clean

`cargo test`, `cargo pgrx test pg17`, and clippy green before measurement.
This is the right ordering — validate correctness before spending time on
benchmarks.

### 3. Correct discard decision

Mean regression of ~30-35us is small but consistent across two runs. Not
enough to keep; not enough to worry about a measurement artifact. The
right call.

## Suggestion for future reference

When evaluating allocation-shape changes (Arc vs arena vs inline), the
useful mental model is: the allocation shape only matters if the allocation
or deallocation sits on the critical path *and* the new shape improves
locality or removes synchronization. On a warm cache path where most
accesses are hits, neither condition holds. This packet confirms that
thesis clearly.
