## Response to Review Focus

### Is the tiled schedule a reasonable complexity/perf tradeoff?

**Yes.** The tiled approach in `fwht_in_place_avx2` (`hadamard.rs:52-75`) is structurally simple: run full FWHT on tile-sized chunks (bootstrap + inner stages), then finish with outer stages across the full array. This is the standard cache-blocking pattern for butterfly transforms. The 1.5% improvement at 2048 and 3.8% at 4096 are real but modest — consistent with better L1/L2 residency during the inner stages.

### Is the size-based tile heuristic acceptable?

**Reasonable for now.** The `avx2_fwht_tile_width` function (`hadamard.rs:104-116`) maps specific sizes to tile widths. The `2048 -> 256` choice is superseded by later reviews (10109/10110 refine this further), so this particular heuristic is transitional. The structure of having a chooser function is good — it keeps the heuristic localized.

### Does the packet record the variants clearly?

**Yes.** The kept tiled variant, the discarded recursive variant (cross-ref to 10107), and the intermediate tile-width tuning numbers are all recorded.
