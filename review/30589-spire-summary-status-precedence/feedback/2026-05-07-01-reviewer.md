# Summary status precedence review

Closes cross-cutting finding #2 from the 30584 closeout.
`SpireRemoteCountRollup::summary_status(top_k, mode)` is the
single derivation function; the four summaries (request,
readiness, execution, libpq request) now route through it. The
precedence test pins the ordering. No blocking findings.

## Confirmations

1. Status derivation logic that 30583 patched in two places is
   now in one place. Future precedence changes touch one
   `match` in one helper, not N open-coded if/else chains.
2. `SpireRemoteSummaryStatusMode` enum makes the per-summary
   variations explicit (e.g. `RequestPlan` vs. `Readiness` vs.
   `Execution` vs. `LibpqRequest`) without duplicating code.
3. Behavior-preserving: SQL-visible values unchanged.

## Findings (non-blocking)

- **Two more summaries still derive status inline.**
  `merge_input_summary_row` (still has its own degraded check
  from 30583) and `finalization_summary_row` were not migrated.
  Reasonable scope choice for this slice — they have additional
  precedence logic (degraded-vs-blocked propagation across
  composed summaries) that may not fit the rollup helper's
  current shape. Track for a future extension to
  `summary_status` that takes a composed-summary mode.
