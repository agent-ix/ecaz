# 30730 — SPIRE production heap handoff contract review

Validation pass on `f92cec90` (`Document SPIRE production heap
handoff contract`). Closes packet 30728 P3 #1 and P3 #2.

## What I checked

- `plan/design/spire-production-coordinator-executor.md` records
  the **monotonic state-extension pattern** for future executor
  stages: pending → sent → ready → failed counter shape used by
  transport and candidate receive should be reused by C2 cancel,
  C3 cache reuse, and C4 strict/degraded extensions. This closes
  30728 P3 #1.
- The C5 section pins the **CandidateReceiveReady-only handoff**
  into Stage D remote heap resolution: only ready dispatches with
  validated candidate batches participate in heap handoff; AM
  scan integration may prove ordered compact-candidate merge but
  final SQL rows must surface `requires_remote_heap_resolution`
  until Stage D resolves origin-node heap visibility. This closes
  30728 P3 #2.
- No code paths changed; this is design-doc only.

## Answers to the packet's review questions

- **Stage-extension pattern leaves enough room for C2 cancellation
  and C4 strict/degraded states?** Yes. Pending/sent/ready/failed
  is a counter shape, not a state-name commitment. C2 will likely
  add cancellation as a *transition* on each existing state
  (`TransportReady → Cancelled`, `CandidateReceiveReady → Cancelled`,
  etc.) rather than a new sibling state, and C4 will add
  `failure_action` mapping per stage. Both extend the pattern
  without breaking it.
- **`CandidateReceiveReady` → Stage D handoff specific enough to
  avoid duplicated compact-receive bookkeeping in AM scan
  integration?** Yes, with packet 30731 layered on top: 30731 has
  the executor own the candidate batch on the ready dispatch and
  exposes the merge helper, so C5 reads from the executor rather
  than maintaining parallel state. The heap-handoff contract here
  ensures C5 cannot prematurely declare final-row readiness while
  Stage D heap resolution is still pending.
