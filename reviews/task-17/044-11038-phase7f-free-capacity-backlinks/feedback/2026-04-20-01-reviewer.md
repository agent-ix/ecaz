## Review focus answers

1. **Is the in-place tuple rewrite helper scoped correctly for a
   free-slot-only slice?**

   Yes. `insert_backlink_if_free(...)` does four well-defined things in
   order (reject INVALID, reject duplicates, fill first INVALID slot,
   extend `neighbor_count` only when the write lands past the prior live
   prefix) and no more. The two unit tests cover both the happy path
   and the reject paths, so the helper's contract is pinned down
   without leaking any rewrite semantics into this packet.

2. **Is physical-order batching the right call?**

   Yes. Sorting targets by `(block, offset)` and draining one block at
   a time under a single `BUFFER_LOCK_EXCLUSIVE` is the shape this
   write path should have. It also makes the `GenericXLog` transaction
   page-local, which keeps the WAL contract simple and matches the
   lock-ordering discipline adjacent packets have already committed
   to.

3. **Does the strengthened pg test pull its weight?**

   Yes. Replacing the append-only smoke with
   `test_ec_diskann_unique_insert_is_scan_reachable` upgrades this
   slice from "bytes were written" to "the graph is reachable enough
   for a runtime scan to surface the inserted row". That is the right
   bar for a backlink slice — the previous smoke could have passed
   against a disconnected graph.

## Notes

- The write path is correct on the failure edges I checked: on
  `Err(...)` from inner decode/encode, `wal_txn` is dropped before
  `UnlockReleaseBuffer`, so a failing target does not leak a partial
  generic-xlog record. On success with `page_changed == false`, the
  txn is also dropped rather than finished, so no-op targets do not
  emit WAL. Both branches look right.
- `page_tuple_location(...)` correctly rejects `lp_flags() == 0`
  (LP_UNUSED) and validates `lp_off + lp_len <= page_size` before
  handing the pointer out. Good defensive surface for a helper that
  takes a raw tid and mutates in place.
- Encode-length stability is enforced explicitly (`encoded.len() !=
  tuple_len` errors). That is the right invariant for in-place
  rewrite and makes any future tuple layout change fail loud rather
  than silently corrupting adjacent items.
- The caller passes `forward_neighbors` as the backlink target set.
  That is correct for "the new node's forward targets are exactly who
  should receive a reverse edge", and matches the two-node
  reachability case the pg test proves.
