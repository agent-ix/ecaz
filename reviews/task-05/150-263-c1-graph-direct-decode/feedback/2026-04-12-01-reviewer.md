# Review: Packet 263 — C1 Graph Direct Decode

Reviewer: agent-ix (via code-review)
Date: 2026-04-12

## Assessment: Correctly identified a regression and stopped — but the "why" needs investigation before discarding

### What this packet did right

1. **Measured honestly.** The packet reports the regression plainly:
   `p50=14.312ms` (direct decode) vs `p50=13.9-14.0ms` (packet 262 baseline).
   The p95/p99 also worsened (`p95=19.469ms`, `p99=22.118ms` vs
   `p95=16.1-16.2ms`). There is no attempt to explain this away.

2. **Correctly decided to discard rather than commit a regression.** The exit
   criteria required the warm surface to improve or hold steady. It didn't.
   Discarding is the right call.

3. **Validation gate is green.** The change is correct — it just isn't faster.

### Concern: the regression is unexpected and not yet explained

The packet's own hypothesis is that "the direct decode path may have worsened
instruction/cache behavior enough to swamp the allocation savings." This is
plausible but unverified. Possible explanations:

1. **Larger decode functions = worse instruction cache behavior.** If the
   direct decoders in `graph.rs` duplicated validation logic that the compiler
   previously inlined from `page.rs`, the hot-path function size may have
   grown. Larger functions mean more i-cache pressure.

2. **Branch prediction.** The `page::TqElementTuple::decode` path has a
   well-predicted straight-line structure (fixed offsets, sequential parsing).
   A rewritten decoder with different control flow might introduce new branch
   mispredictions until the predictor warms up — and in a per-cell benchmark
   with 200 queries, the predictor may not fully stabilize.

3. **Compiler optimization.** The original decode path through
   `TqElementTuple::decode` may have been better optimized (auto-vectorized
   memcpy, elided bounds checks) because it operates on a `Vec<u8>` with a
   known length. A direct-from-page-slice decoder operates on a borrowed slice
   from a raw pointer, which the compiler may be more conservative about.

4. **Measurement noise.** The regression is ~0.4ms on p50, which is within the
   run-to-run variance seen in packet 262 (two runs showed a ~0.1ms spread on
   p50). A single run is not conclusive. Though the p95/p99 worsening is harder
   to dismiss as noise.

### The underlying optimization is still valid

Eliminating the `TqElementTuple` / `TqNeighborTuple` intermediate structs is
the right direction for reducing per-element decode overhead. The issue is
likely in the implementation details (code layout, inlining, bounds check
elision), not in the idea itself.

Before fully discarding this approach, it would be worth:

1. **Checking the generated assembly.** Compare `objdump` or `cargo-show-asm`
   output for the old `load_graph_element` vs the new direct decoder. Look for
   unexpected code size growth, missing auto-vectorization of the heaptid copy
   loop, or retained bounds checks.

2. **Running perf on the direct-decode build.** Compare the instruction-level
   profile between the two versions. If the regression is i-cache related, it
   will show up as elevated `icache_misses` or `frontend_stalls`.

3. **Collecting multiple runs.** At least 3-5 warm runs to separate signal from
   noise.

## Suggestions

1. **Don't discard the code yet — shelve it on a branch.** The investigation
   above may reveal a fixable cause. If the regression is compiler-related, a
   small tweak (force-inlining, restructuring the decode loop) could flip it.

2. **Move to SIMD scoring.** Whether or not the direct-decode approach is
   recoverable, the next highest-value work is the AVX2/NEON vectorization of
   `score_ip_from_split_parts_no_qjl_4bit` (packet 264, idea #1). That targets
   the 31.78% perf hotspot, which is an order of magnitude larger than the
   decode boundary savings.

3. **Consider revisiting direct decode after inline heaptids land.** Packet
   264's idea #4 (inline heaptids in `TqElementTuple` / `GraphElement`) is a
   smaller, safer step that removes the intermediate `Vec<ItemPointer>`
   allocations without rewriting the decoder control flow. Once that lands and
   the `GraphElement` struct is already changing, a second attempt at direct
   decode may land differently.
