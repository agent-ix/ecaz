# Review: Packet 262 — C1 Graph Tuple Copy Boundary

Reviewer: agent-ix (via code-review)
Date: 2026-04-12

## Assessment: Good experimental discipline — the right probe landed and the wrong probe was correctly discarded

### What this packet did right

1. **Tried the narrowest thing first, measured, discarded when flat.** The
   `SelectedScanResult` inline heap-tid change was the smallest possible
   materialization tweak. Measuring it against the honest warm surface and
   finding it flat (`p50=14.176ms` vs `14.315ms`) is useful negative signal.
   Discarding it instead of committing marginal complexity is the right call.

   This validates the recommendation in packet 264's survey: the heap-tid copy
   in scan-result materialization is ~60 bytes per emission, 10 times for
   LIMIT 10. It was always expected to be noise-level.

2. **The graph decode change is directionally correct.** Removing the
   `read_page_tuple_bytes(...).to_vec()` intermediate copy eliminates one heap
   allocation + 772-byte memcpy per element load. The 1-2% p50 improvement
   (`14.315ms` -> `13.9-14.0ms`) is small but real and consistent across two
   runs.

3. **Validation gate is green.** All three checkpoints pass. Good.

### Concern: the p99 variance is wide

Run 1 shows `p99=20.743ms` vs baseline `p99=17.613ms`. Run 2 shows
`p99=17.652ms` (matching baseline). This suggests either:

- Background system noise on the WSL2 host during run 1
- The change shifted a cache/allocation pattern that causes occasional
  higher-latency outliers

Two runs is not enough to distinguish these. If this packet lands on `main`,
a follow-up should collect at least 5 warm runs to confirm the p99 is stable.
The p50/mean are consistent enough to commit, but the p99 claim should be
hedged.

### Observation: diminishing returns on copy-boundary squeezing

This packet + the discarded probe together show that owned-copy removal on the
graph/scan materialization path yields single-digit-percent warm improvements
at best. The remaining copies (heaptid Vecs inside `TqElementTuple::decode`,
`code.to_vec()` inside the same decode) are still present but are now second-
order costs.

The survey in packet 264 estimates these allocation-focused wins at ~1-2ms
total if all of them land. That's real but not sufficient — the 3x gap to
NFR-001 needs the SIMD scoring win first, which targets the 31.78% hotspot
rather than the ~4% allocation tail.

## Suggestions

1. **Merge the graph decode change.** The 1-2% p50 win is small but clean and
   free of risk. Don't hold it for a larger batch.

2. **Shift focus to SIMD scoring next.** The copy-boundary work has reached the
   point of diminishing returns. Packet 264's idea #1 (AVX2/NEON for
   `score_ip_from_split_parts_no_qjl_4bit`) is the highest-signal next step.

3. **Collect p99 stability data.** Run the warm surface 5 times before and
   after to confirm the p99 isn't regressing.
