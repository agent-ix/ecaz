# Priority redirect: pivot from HNSW to IVF / SPIRE / quant-RABITQ surface

## Decision

Stop scheduling new HNSW packets. The next Task 35 slices should target
`ec_ivf`, `ec_spire`, and the RABITQ-adjacent quant surface
(`src/quant/hadamard.rs`, RABITQ-feeding code paths) until those three
surfaces are at parity with HNSW's current coverage.

## Why

Reviewer priority for safing the codebase, this conversation, 2026-05-18:
IVF, SPIRE, and RABITQ are the surfaces that need protection coverage first.
The current Task 35 packet sequence (HNSW → IVF → SPIRE in the task doc)
does not match that priority.

Coverage gap as of HEAD = `75ebce93` (after packet 020 land):

- `ec_hnsw`: 1,020 baseline entries, 9 packets landed (012–020).
- `ec_spire`: 886 baseline entries, only packet 007 has touched it, and
  007 only covers `lib.rs` SPIRE entrypoints — none of the SPIRE module
  internals (`dml_frontdoor/mod.rs` 159, `coordinator/*` 200+, `page.rs`
  58, `storage/*` 65, `custom_scan/*` 90+, `vacuum/mod.rs` 34) are
  documented yet.
- `ec_ivf`: 326 baseline entries, zero packets directly. All of
  `page.rs` (133), `scan.rs` (101), `build.rs` (23), `vacuum.rs` (26),
  `insert.rs` (21), `admin.rs` (10), `cost.rs` (4), `options.rs` (8)
  are untouched.
- `src/quant`: 74 baseline entries, zero packets directly. `hadamard.rs`
  is 62 of those and feeds the RABITQ pipeline; `prod.rs` is 12.

HNSW residual work is real, but it is no longer the highest-leverage
surface for reviewer confidence.

## Suggested packet sequence going forward

Roughly highest-impact-first within each priority surface; coder may
re-order based on what reads as one coherent slice.

### Quant / RABITQ adjacency

1. `src/quant/hadamard.rs` (62 entries) — pure SIMD, no FFI, predictable
   `_mm256_loadu_ps` / `_mm256_storeu_ps` contracts; one packet.
2. `src/quant/prod.rs` (12 entries) — small, close out the quant surface.

### IVF

3. `src/am/ec_ivf/cost.rs` (4) + `options.rs` (8) + `admin.rs` (10) — a
   small bundled slice of planner-callback + options + admin boundaries
   to seed the IVF lane.
4. `src/am/ec_ivf/page.rs` (133) — largest IVF file; page-layout /
   tuple-pointer arithmetic; one focused packet.
5. `src/am/ec_ivf/scan.rs` (101) — IVF scan callbacks and posting-list
   traversal; analogous to the HNSW scan pattern already worked.
6. `src/am/ec_ivf/build.rs` (23) + `insert.rs` (21) + `vacuum.rs` (26)
   — bundle the maintenance paths if they share contracts.

### SPIRE

7. `src/am/ec_spire/scan/callbacks.rs` (4) + `coordinator/lifecycle.rs`
   (6) + `coordinator/diagnostics.rs` (9) + small `remote_candidates/*`
   files — small surfaces first to establish SPIRE patterns.
8. `src/am/ec_spire/scan/relation.rs` (31) + `vacuum/mod.rs` (34) +
   `cost/mod.rs` (22) — mid-size SPIRE entries on familiar
   relation-resource patterns (consume Task 41 wrappers).
9. `src/am/ec_spire/storage/relation_store.rs` (51) + `page.rs` (58).
10. `src/am/ec_spire/coordinator/snapshots.rs` (62) +
    `hierarchy_snapshots.rs` (71).
11. `src/am/ec_spire/custom_scan/*` (~150 across multiple files).
12. `src/am/ec_spire/dml_frontdoor/mod.rs` (159) — largest single
    SPIRE file; likely needs subdivision into 2-3 packets.

### Return to HNSW residual after the above

Once IVF + SPIRE + quant are at coverage parity, sweep the remaining
HNSW files (`build.rs` 33, `vacuum.rs`, `insert.rs`, `routine.rs`, etc.).
