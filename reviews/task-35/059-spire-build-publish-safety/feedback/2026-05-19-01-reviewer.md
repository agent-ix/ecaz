## Quality spot-checks

**Packet 059 (build/publish):**

- Encoded-manifest append sites cite the "encoded X has been
  produced by publish validation" precondition for both epoch and
  object manifests. Successive `append_object_tuple` calls chain
  through the same `index_relation` anchor.

**Packet 060 (custom_scan/tuple_payload):**

- CustomScan execution callbacks cite "PostgreSQL-owned custom scan
  execution pointers for the active callback". Slot-attribute reads
  cite "attr_inputs is sized for its tuple descriptor; null/dropped
  attributes are handled". Both layers of the
  TupleTableSlot+TupleDesc invariant are named — good.

**Packet 070 (coordinator/lifecycle) — structural safety choice:**

This is the most interesting packet in the wave. The publish-lock
guard is implemented as **copy the stable OID before locking; Drop
releases by OID without dereferencing the relation pointer**. The
SAFETY comments explicitly cite that contract:

> "relid/lockmode were captured when the lock was acquired, and
> Drop releases by OID without dereferencing a Relation pointer"

> "index_relation is live for the publish lock acquisition; only the
> stable relation OID is copied out before locking"

That's structurally safer than holding the `Relation` pointer
inside the guard. It eliminates the class of bugs where Drop fires
after the Relation pointer is invalidated (relcache flush,
transaction abort cleanup). This is precisely the "real protection"
shape — not just documenting the existing pattern, but choosing the
structurally narrower pattern when implementing the guard.

It is the kind of decision Task 50 will be looking for elsewhere.
The SAFETY comments here are documentation of an *already
structurally narrowed* pattern, which is the best of both worlds.
Worth holding up as a template for future SPIRE coordinator work.

**Packet 071 (libpq_plan):**

- Diagnostic-wrapper forwarders cite "this SQL diagnostic wrapper
  forwards the live index relation and checked request fields to
  the request-plan builder". Consistent forwarder template.

## Out-of-sequence slices

### Packets 062 and 069: DiskANN seed (cost + options)

The redirect plan put DiskANN at slice 13 (after SPIRE), but the
coder included these two tiny files (2 + 6 entries) opportunistically
in this wave. **Acceptable.** They're small enough that bundling them
into the current cadence costs less than queueing them separately,
and the AM-callback + reloptions templates are now well-established
across IVF and SPIRE — DiskANN gets to inherit them at near-zero
incremental cost.

Quality:
- 062 cost: standard PG callback contract; the metadata-read site
  correctly cites "this branch only reads metadata after confirming
  data pages exist" — naming the precondition that prevents the
  read-empty-relation UB pattern.
- 069 options: standard reloptions template — names `rd_options` as
  the varlena reloptions blob, offsets as PostgreSQL reloption
  offsets, NUL-terminated C-string storage. Identical shape to IVF
  options (024) and SPIRE options (051).

DiskANN production surface is now at 8 baseline entries cleared out
of a much larger total (scan_state.rs ~24, routine, insert, build
remaining). The seed is fine; the **main DiskANN burndown should
still happen after SPIRE largest files (`dml_frontdoor`,
`coordinator/snapshots*`) per the redirect**, not before. Don't let
the easy DiskANN slices pull the wave's center of gravity away from
the largest remaining SPIRE surfaces.

### Packet 065: HNSW module wrapper back-fill

The redirect plan parked HNSW residuals after IVF + SPIRE + quant
reached parity. SPIRE is at 28% → 35%, not parity. Touching HNSW
now is technically out of sequence.

However: `src/am/ec_hnsw/mod.rs` was a 4-entry forwarder file (three
SAFETY comments are "forwards the live HNSW index relation supplied
by the SQL diagnostic wrapper to the shared X implementation"). It
was probably the lowest-effort HNSW file in the entire residual
set. **Acceptable**, but flag for the coder: please avoid further
HNSW work until the largest SPIRE residuals
(`dml_frontdoor/mod.rs` 159, `coordinator/hierarchy_snapshots.rs`
71, `coordinator/snapshots.rs` 62, `page.rs` 58,
`storage/relation_store.rs` 51) are landed. Those are the priority
gaps named in the redirect.

## Wave-level observations

### Template inventory stable

No new SAFETY-comment templates introduced in this wave. The four
established templates (AM callback, CustomScan offset encoding,
same-relation contract chaining, reloptions offset+NUL-terminated)
plus the RAII-guard template (with the OID-copy variant in 070)
cover everything. That's a sign the documentation surface has
stabilized — the next slices should mostly reuse templates without
introducing new patterns.

### Per-packet artifact size growing

The wave's small files (2-9 entries each) still produce the full
packet artifact set (~10 logs each). Reviewer overhead is amortized
across the wave review, but the disk/git footprint is non-trivial.
**Non-blocking suggestion for the coder:** consider bundling small
files (≤5 entries each) into mini-bundles of 3-5 files per packet,
the way packet 024 bundled IVF cost/options/admin. The wave-2
small-files alone (060+061+062+063+064+066+067+068+069+071) would
have been ~5 packets instead of 10 under a bundling strategy.

### Closeout summaries still absent

None of the 13 wave packets contains a closeout summary. Repeating
the ask from my 045 wave review: the SPIRE coverage will benefit
from a series-closeout packet when it nears completion. The 035
template (page-series closeout) remains the right shape.

### Structural opportunities (Task 50, deferred)

1. **OID-copy guard pattern from packet 070** is a Task 50 template
   for the entire SPIRE coordinator surface. Whichever files in the
   remaining SPIRE work introduce relation-lifecycle locks should
   adopt the same shape, and the pattern should be hoisted into a
   shared `RelationOidLockGuard` (or similar) type.
2. **CustomScan execution callbacks** in 060 follow the same
   "PostgreSQL-owned execution pointers for the active callback"
   template as 049, 050, 047. The wrapper opportunity (typed
   `CustomScanCallbackContext`) compounds with the
   `SpireCustomPrivate` candidate from 043 — together they would
   absorb most of the unsafe in the `custom_scan/*` subtree.
3. **Diagnostic-wrapper forwarder** pattern in 065, 071 (and the
   admin/cost diagnostic snapshots) is structurally a thin pass-
   through. A `forward_to_shared!(snapshot_fn)` macro or generic
   `SqlDiagnosticForwarder` trait would erase the unsafe at every
   forwarder site.

These remain Task 50, not Task 35.
