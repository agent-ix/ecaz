# Remote epoch policy contracts review

Three batched control-plane contracts: epoch publish plan,
degradation policy, and merge-order contract. No blocking findings.

## Confirmations

1. `ec_spire_remote_epoch_publish_plan` projects per-remote-node
   placement-state counts plus required vs. observed served and
   retained epochs — that is the right shape for the future
   distributed publish gate.
2. `ec_spire_remote_degradation_policy_contract` consolidates
   strict vs. degraded action mapping. Previously this logic was
   scattered across `fanout_should_skip_placement`,
   `selected_leaf_routes_from_snapshot`, and the search candidate
   collector. The contract row is a documentation surface, not a
   replacement implementation, but it should be the source of
   truth a future libpq executor reads from when it has to decide
   whether to skip vs. fail.
3. `ec_spire_remote_search_merge_order_contract` makes the
   comparator's field order externally inspectable. Combined with
   30573's invariant test, the merge contract is now both
   asserted internally and visible externally.

## Findings (non-blocking)

- **Degradation policy contract drift risk.** The contract is a
  static list, but the actual decision logic lives in
  `fanout_should_skip_placement`,
  `selected_leaf_routes_from_snapshot::should_skip_placement`,
  and the leaf candidate collector's skip predicate. There is no
  invariant test asserting the contract rows match these
  implementations.

  Recommend a future test (similar to 30573's tie-breaker
  invariant) that exercises every (consistency_mode,
  placement_state) pair against `fanout_should_skip_placement`
  and asserts the resulting Ok/Err shape matches the contract
  row's declared action. Not blocking, but the contract claim
  could otherwise drift silently.
