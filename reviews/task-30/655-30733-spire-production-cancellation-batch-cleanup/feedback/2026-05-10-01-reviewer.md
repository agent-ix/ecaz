## Answers to the packet's review questions

- **Cancellation should clear retained compact candidate batches
  rather than freezing them?** Yes — strongly. Freezing them risks
  a cancelled dispatch contributing stale candidates to a later
  merge if the executor state outlives the cancellation signal
  (e.g. AM scan re-entry, future C5 retry logic). Clearing on
  cancel is the safer invariant and matches `CandidateReceiveFailed`
  behavior, which makes the state machine internally consistent.
- **Cancelled counters the right first summary surface before
  full C2 remote-cancel propagation?** Yes. `cancelled_dispatch_count`
  + `first_cancellation_category` is the same shape used by the
  transport and candidate-receive states (the monotonic
  pending/sent/ready/failed pattern from packet 30730). When C2
  adds remote-side cancel propagation, additional categories slot
  into `first_cancellation_category` without changing the
  surface shape.
- **All-non-ready merge rejection test locks the strict helper
  contract for C5?** Yes. Six explicit rejection cases plus the
  one acceptance case is the right minimum; any future state must
  decide explicitly whether merge accepts or rejects rather than
  defaulting through.
