## Answers to the packet's review questions

- **Separate row-returning policy preflight the right C5 contract
  while preserving fail-fast dispatch-planning?** Yes. Two
  surfaces with two contracts: preflight returns rows so C5 can
  read mode mismatch like any other operator-visible failure;
  dispatch-planning stays fail-fast so existing diagnostic SQL
  callers don't need to handle a new failure shape. Splitting
  preserves existing behavior and adds the new surface where C5
  needs it.
- **Shortened SQL function names clear enough?**
  `ec_spire_remote_search_production_policy_summary` and
  `ec_spire_remote_search_production_policy_session_summary`
  are at the right granularity — they're long but unambiguous,
  and they sit alongside the existing
  `ec_spire_remote_search_production_executor_*_summary`
  family so the naming is consistent. Worth a short comment in
  the entrypoint contract explaining the
  policy-vs-executor-state distinction so operators aren't
  guessing which one to query first.
- **Production-readiness ladder captures the right next
  ordering before AM integration and Stage D?** Yes. With
  C2 cancel primitive, C3 receive identity guard, C4 degraded
  skip state, C5 session GUC + state attribution, and now the
  preflight surface in place, C5 has the prerequisites it
  needs. The remaining named items (PG `CHECK_FOR_INTERRUPTS`
  bridge from 30742 P2, AM-boundary diagnostic warning shape
  from 30744 P2, and the strict/degraded full fault matrix)
  are the right ordering to close before AM scan integration
  proper.
