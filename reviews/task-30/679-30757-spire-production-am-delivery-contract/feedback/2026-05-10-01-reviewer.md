- The summary surface from packet 30755 now consumes the AM

  returns both heap-resolution status and AM-delivery
  readiness.
- Fail-closed posture verified: any remote-origin row in the
  top-k stream → `requires_remote_row_materialization` for
  the **whole** delivery, not a partial local prefix. This

  `xs_heaptid`. The classification rejects that case at the
  contract level rather than relying on every consumer to
  remember the rule.

## Answers to the packet's review questions

- **Fail-closed rule correct when merged top-k contains any
  remote-origin output?** Yes — definitively. The alternative
  ("return the local prefix") would either:
  1. silently truncate results (operator sees a smaller-than-
     requested top-k with no signal that remote rows were
     dropped), or
  2. require re-merging after dropping remotes (which would
     change the ordering and break the deterministic tie-break
     from packet 30731).
  Neither is acceptable. Fail-closed with a named blocker is
  the only safe behavior until packet 30758 / future slices
  land remote row materialization.
- **`node_id == SPIRE_LOCAL_NODE_ID` plus `coordinator_local_heap`
  a sufficient predicate for outputs that may become
  `xs_heaptid`?** Yes. `SPIRE_LOCAL_NODE_ID` is the canonical
  marker for the coordinator-local placement (line 935, 1015,
  5208 references all use it consistently). A remote node's
  output cannot accidentally match because its `node_id` is by
  construction non-local (descriptor-registered remotes have
  distinct `node_id` per packets 30683/30710).
- **`remote_row_materialization` the right next blocker name
  and boundary?** Yes. It names *what's missing* (a
  materialization contract that turns remote heap-resolved
  rows into PostgreSQL tuples) rather than *what failed*.
  Operators reading the blocker will know the next slice's
  scope without ambiguity. Joins the `remote_heap_resolution`
  / `remote_endpoint_identity` / `remote_executor_governance`
  next-blocker family from packets 30708/30711/30717 with the
  same naming convention.
