# Descriptor registration contract review

`ec_spire_remote_node_descriptor_registration_contract()` exposes the
9-step registration flow with required validators, persistence
actions, and failure statuses. Addresses the 30574 follow-up about
`remote_index_regclass` resolution clarity. No blocking findings.

## Confirmations

1. Each contract row has `validator` + `persistence_action` +
   `failure_status` columns — operators get a precise mapping
   between a registration step and what fails when it does not
   pass.
2. `must_advance_descriptor_generation` (step 9) and
   `atomically_replace_descriptor` are documented as the final
   gate, which is the right ordering — earlier steps validate,
   final step commits.
3. Connection details remain indirect:
   `persist_secret_reference_only` is the explicit persistence
   action for `conninfo_secret_name`.

## Findings

- **The contract is now a SQL claim that 30595's persistence
  layer must back.** See 30595 feedback — the upsert helper does
  not yet enforce `must_advance_descriptor_generation` or filter
  by `descriptor_state` on the readout, so two of the contract's
  promises are not currently honored at the persistence boundary.
