## Answers to the packet's review questions

- **Local-cancellation summary correctly modeled as query-
  wide in both strict and degraded mode?** Yes —
  definitively. A local-cancel is a coordinator-side
  event that affects the entire query; allowing degraded
  mode to skip past it (treating it as a per-node
  failure) would contradict the operator's cancel signal.
  Both modes returning identical cancelled-state
  summaries is the correct semantics.
- **Appropriate for `local_statement_timeout` to share
  the same executor-cancellation path while preserving
  distinct category?** Yes. They have different *origins*
  (operator cancel vs PostgreSQL local statement timeout)
  but identical *effects* on the executor (cancel all
  dispatches). The shared path with category
  preservation matches what operators need: "the query
  was cancelled" + "here's specifically why."
- **Fixture narrow enough for Stage E without claiming
  final AM result delivery?** Yes — the harness asserts
  cancellation state at the executor-summary layer, not
  AM tuple delivery.
