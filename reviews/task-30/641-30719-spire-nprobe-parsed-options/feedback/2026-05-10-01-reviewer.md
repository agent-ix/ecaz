## Answers to the packet's review questions

- **`Option<Vec<u32>>` the right narrow fix vs the fixed-array
  `SpireRecursiveNprobePolicy` shape?** The `Option<Vec<u32>>`
  shape is right for *this* slice. A fixed-array variant
  (`[u32; MAX_NPROBE_PER_LEVEL_ENTRIES]` + `len`, which exists
  already at line 269 for the snapshot path) would couple
  reloption parsing to the recursive policy compile-time bound
  and force every reloption read to validate length there. The
  current shape keeps the bound check at policy-construction time
  where it belongs. If a future packet measures the small Vec
  clone as a hot-path cost, moving to the fixed array is a clean
  follow-on.
- **Cloning accessor acceptable for this stage?** Yes. The clone
  is a small Vec over u32 (max 16 entries by the policy bound),
  amortized over scan planning rather than per-tuple work. Parse
  cost is gone, which was the F-NPROBE-1 concern. A
  `&[u32]` accessor would shave the clone but tightens lifetime
  discipline across callers; not worth doing pre-emptively.
- **Phase 3 fanout-vs-nprobe note resolves the closeout ambiguity
  without prematurely flipping the fanout checkbox?** Yes. The
  note records the semantic split without claiming the build-time
  fanout work is closed; F-NPROBE-2 was a documentation issue
  rather than a missing implementation, and this resolves it
  cleanly.
