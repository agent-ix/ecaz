## Review focus answers

> Confirm the score accumulation contract is the right first global
> frontier.

Yes ‚Äî sum-of-IP-scores across levels is the correct first global
frontier under the existing IP comparator. Top-graph distances entering
as `-distance` preserves the same comparator. Document it explicitly
(F3).

> Is `max_routing_expansions = max(active_leaf_count, beam_width)` the
> right finite guardrail?

Right *shape* (finite, leaf-count-aware), generous *value* (rarely
fires in practice). Acceptable as a first cut. Tighten under Phase 9.4
remaining diagnostics work or under Phase 10 cost-model integration.
See F1.

> Does preserving `nprobe_per_level` as the local per-parent fanout
> input while making `beam_width`/`max_leaf_routes` the global caps
> match Phase 9 architecture intent?

Yes ‚Äî `nprobe_per_level` controls *per-parent* expansion (a local
choice) while `beam_width` / `max_leaf_routes` cap *cumulative*
expansion (a global guardrail). The two are orthogonal in the right
way. F2 notes one ambiguity (which `nprobe` value drives the global
cap) worth documenting.

> Diagnostics that should be added before closing the remaining 9.4
> diagnostics checkbox.

Suggested per-level rows: `level`, `parents_visited`,
`children_expanded`, `leaf_routes_emitted`, `truncation_reason`. The
existing `truncation_reason` in `SpireRoutedLeafScanRows` covers the
final cap; per-level visibility lets operators distinguish
"beam_width capped at level 3" from "max_leaf_routes capped at the
end."
