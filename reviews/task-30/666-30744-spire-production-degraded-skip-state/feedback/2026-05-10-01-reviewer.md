## Answers to the packet's review questions

- **`DegradedSkipped` the right explicit state for partial remote
  failures before AM scan integration?** Yes. A separate state
  (rather than a flag on `*Failed`) keeps the strict and degraded
  reasoning paths uniform: every state-machine consumer (merge,
  summary, future Stage D handoff) sees `DegradedSkipped` as a
  first-class state and decides explicitly whether to handle or
  ignore it.
- **Should degraded skips stay out of
  `transport_failed_dispatch_count` and
  `candidate_receive_failed_dispatch_count`, with their own skip
  counter and first category?** Yes. Mixing degraded skips into
  the failure counters would lose the distinction operators need:
  "did the executor fail closed or did it skip nodes per
  policy?" The separate counters preserve that distinction in
  one query.
- **Compact merge ignores degraded-skipped dispatches and errors
  only on non-ready, non-skipped states?** Yes. Ignoring
  degraded-skipped is the correct merge policy because the
  operator has explicitly opted into "best-effort with whatever
  ready remotes responded." The merge helper continues to reject
  the *non-ready, non-skipped* states (planned, transport-ready,
  transport-failed, candidate-receive-failed, cancelled,
  blocked) because those are inconsistent with merge readiness.

## P2 — close before AM scan integration (C5)

1. **AM-boundary policy.** The C4 work currently lands inside
   the executor state machine; AM scan integration must decide
   whether the AM-level consistency mode (strict / degraded)
   maps to the executor's per-dispatch decisions or to a
   query-wide setting. Recommend the C5 packet pin:
   - the AM scan reads consistency mode from a session GUC or
     query option (named explicitly);
   - the GUC value is threaded into the executor as a single
     `consistency_mode: SpireConsistencyMode` rather than per
     dispatch;
   - degraded mode at the AM boundary returns final SQL rows
     accompanied by a diagnostic warning naming the skipped
     nodes (or at minimum the count + first-skip-category).
   Without that contract, downstream callers won't know whether
   a "successful" SQL row set was produced from full or partial
   remotes.
