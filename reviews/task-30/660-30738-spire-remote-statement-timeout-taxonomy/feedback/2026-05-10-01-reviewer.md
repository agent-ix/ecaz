## Answers to the packet's review questions

- **`remote_statement_timeout` the right category for remote
  SQLSTATE `57014` with a statement-timeout message?** Yes.
  SQLSTATE `57014` is `query_canceled` in general but a remote
  `statement_timeout` is the most common cause and has its own
  operator remediation (raise the per-remote timeout vs.
  investigate cancellation provenance). Distinguishing the
  cause-by-message refinement is the right call for the
  diagnostic surface; if the message doesn't indicate
  statement_timeout, falling back to `remote_query_cancelled` (or
  `remote_query_failed` until that category claims it) keeps the
  taxonomy honest.
- **Reserving `remote_query_cancelled` and
  `remote_backend_terminated` useful without over-claiming local
  cancel propagation?** Yes. Reserving the category names now
  prevents the C2 local-cancel slice from inventing a competing
  string later, and lets 30739 claim
  `remote_backend_terminated` cleanly without back-fitting an
  enum.
- **Two PG18 loopback fixtures cover both async transport and
  compact-candidate receive paths?** Yes. Receive and transport
  reach the SQLSTATE classifier through different call sites; a
  single fixture would have left a regression hole. Symmetric
  coverage here is the right minimum.

## P3 — informational

1. **Cause-by-message robustness.** The classifier matches on
   message text to disambiguate `57014` between statement_timeout
   and other cancellation causes. Worth pinning the exact
   substring(s) the classifier matches on (and whether they're
   case-sensitive) in a small comment near the constant — the
   message wording is a PostgreSQL-side convention that has been
   stable across versions but isn't part of the SQL standard.
   Same shape as the 30708 P3 #1 concern about substring-based
   classification.

2. **Symmetric reserve for connect timeout.** A `connect_failed`
   category exists from packet 30725, but a *connect_timeout*
   would be operator-distinguishable from a connect refusal. If
   `connect_failed` becomes overloaded, consider reserving
   `remote_connect_timeout` alongside the C2 cancellation
   categories. Optional — only worth doing if real operator
   feedback shows confusion.
