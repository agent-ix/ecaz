## Summary

Pre-checkpoint review accepted (`2026-05-11-001-reviewer.md`).
The now-finalized request.md adds one nuance worth confirming:
"Keeps unsupported baserel shapes as `None` so internal SPI
queries used by the PK-select primitive do not get hijacked by
the DML CustomScan path."

That's a real concern: the PK-select primitive itself runs SPI
SELECTs (e.g., `ec_spire_forward_coordinator_select_tuple_payload`
calls into the placement directory). If those internal SQL
statements hit the planner hook, they'd recurse through the same
extraction path. Returning `None` for "not a candidate" rather
than `Some(Err(...))` keeps internal queries on the standard
plan path. That's the correct semantic distinction:
- `None` = "not for me, pass through cleanly."
- `Some(Err)` = "looked like a candidate but malformed —
  fail closed at planner time."
