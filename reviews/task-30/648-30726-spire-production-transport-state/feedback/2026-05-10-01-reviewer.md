## Answers to the packet's review questions

- **`TransportReady` / `TransportFailed` the right state granularity
  before candidate receive lands?** Yes. Two terminal C1 outcomes
  with named transitions match the row-vs-batch distinction from
  packet 30725. Adding finer states (e.g. `TransportPending`)
  before C2 cancellation lands would create transitions C2 wants
  to own; keeping the slice at two states is the right minimum.
- **Should transport failure dominate the summary status when any
  dispatch fails, or should partial states be represented
  differently before C4 strict/degraded?** For now, surfacing the
  first failure category alongside ready/pending counts is the
  right shape. C4 will replace "first failure dominates" with
  strict-fail-closed vs degraded-skip-by-node logic; until then,
  exposing all four counters lets operators see partial state
  without the executor having to choose a single overall verdict.
- **SQL summary counters sufficient for operators to distinguish
  dry / transport-ready / transport-failed states?** Yes. Pending
  vs ready vs failed counts plus first failure category cover the
  three operationally interesting shapes (dry zero, partial
  ready, partial failed). If a fourth shape appears (e.g.
  cancelled, post-C2), add a counter rather than a status string
  so operator queries don't need to widen.
