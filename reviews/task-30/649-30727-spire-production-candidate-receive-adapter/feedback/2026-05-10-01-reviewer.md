## Answers to the packet's review questions

- **Right adapter boundary for compact candidate receive before
  AM scan wiring?** Yes. Sharing the transport adapter avoids two
  separate connection lifecycles, and reusing the SQL template +
  decoder + validator avoids drift between the diagnostic and
  production paths. The boundary at `run_candidate_receive_requests`
  is the natural seam C5 will plug into.
- **Should candidate decode and batch validation failures remain
  separate failure categories for C4 strict/degraded mapping?**
  Yes. They have different operator remediation paths: a decode
  failure is a coordinator/remote protocol mismatch (suggests
  version skew or a coordinator bug) while a validation failure is
  a contract violation by the remote (wrong fingerprint, malformed
  candidate row, etc.). Folding them into one category in C4
  would lose the distinction. Both should map to strict
  fail-closed and degraded skip_node with different
  `next_blocker` strings.
- **PG18 loopback `rabitq` fixture enough for this test-facing
  receive slice?** Yes for the slice. The fixture exercises the
  full path from request → adapter → SQL → decode → validate →
  result row. Multi-node merge / AM scan integration belong in C5
  with their own fixtures.
