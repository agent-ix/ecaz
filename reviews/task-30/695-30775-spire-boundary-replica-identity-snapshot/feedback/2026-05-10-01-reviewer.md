## Answers to the packet's review questions

- **Is assignment grouping by `vec_id` the right shape for
  checking primary and boundary-replica identity
  consistency?** Yes. ADR-055/063/064 say boundary replicas
  must carry the **same** original-vector identity as their
  primary; grouping by `vec_id` makes a mismatch immediately
  visible (one group with multiple distinct IDs would
  produce two separate rows rather than one consolidated
  row). The diagnostic surfaces both the primary count and
  the boundary-replica count per `vec_id`, so a future
  Stage E fixture can assert "1 primary, N replicas, all
  global" per logical row.
- **`ready`, `local_scope_only`, and `requires_global_vec_id`
  strict enough for Stage E to consume without overclaiming
  remote proof?** Yes. The diagnostic explicitly fences
  itself to "local index-level evidence over active
  placement files" — packet text and the doc both flag that
  remote multi-instance proof remains part of Stage E
  fixture evidence. The three-category split lets the
  fixture know whether the local index is ready to
  participate in cross-node dedupe at all, before it tries.
- **Does the test cover the important local multi-store
  boundary-replica case, or should this diagnostic also
  force a delta-row case before the Stage E fixture?** The
  test covers the populated-build path. A delta-insert
  case (where the boundary replica lands as a delta row
  rather than a leaf row) would exercise the
  `delta_insert_count` field that the row reports — worth
  adding before Stage E fixture work consumes the
  diagnostic. See P3 below.
