## Answers to the packet's review questions

- **`last_served_epoch = 0` against requested epoch `1`
  acceptable local epoch-mismatch injection for this
  pre-dispatch fixture row?** Yes. The injection produces
  the expected `stale_epoch` capability status because
  `last_served_epoch < requested_epoch`, which is the
  exact condition packet 30711's capability gate checks.
  A larger gap (e.g. `last_served_epoch = 0` vs
  `requested_epoch = 100`) would not exercise additional
  behavior — the gate fires on any non-monotonic relation.
  Minimum-viable injection is the right choice.
- **Reusing the version-skew pre-dispatch fixture family
  the right shape for stale epoch windows?** Yes. Per my
  30779 review answer: both failures are pre-dispatch
  capability gate, same executor-state transformation
  (blocked-before-dispatch → degraded-skipped on degraded
  mode), same observation surface. Forking the script
  would have duplicated setup/teardown logic for two
  cases that share every mechanism except the injection
  parameter.
- **Should the next Stage E row stay in pre-dispatch
  capability failures, or move into receive-time endpoint
  identity failures?** Either is reasonable. See P3
  below.
