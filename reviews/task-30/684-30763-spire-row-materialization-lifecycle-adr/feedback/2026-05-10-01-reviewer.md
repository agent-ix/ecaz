## Answers to the packet's review questions

- **Is rejecting per-cursor/temp/scratch proxy rows the
  right interpretation of PostgreSQL's `xs_heaptid` relation
  identity contract?** Yes. PostgreSQL's executor expects
  `xs_heaptid` to identify a row in the relation currently
  being scanned, with normal MVCC visibility, lockability,
  and downstream operator semantics (e.g. row-level locks,
  WAL recoverability for any subsequent write, replication
  identity). A per-cursor temp tuple-store satisfies the
  TID shape but breaks every other expectation downstream
  operators have. The ADR is right to reject it for v1 and
  redirect non-AM tuple delivery to a future FDW/custom-scan
  surface where those constraints don't apply.
- **Is epoch-scoped same-relation coordinator heap
  materialization the right v1 lifecycle before
  implementation starts?** Yes. Tying lifetime to the
  epoch/MVCC machinery rather than the scan cursor means:
  - the row is reusable across queries (avoiding repeated
    materialization cost),
  - cleanup happens through the same epoch retirement path
    SPIRE already uses for other materialized state,
  - downstream operators see a normal heap row for the
    duration of the epoch.
  This pushes the materialization implementation into a
  separate maintenance workflow (likely a coordinator-side
  replication-style sync), which is the right separation —
  scan cursors should not own heap-write lifecycle.
- **Are the implementation consequences explicit enough:
  no scan-time heap writes, validate visible same-relation
  materialized TIDs, and keep FDW/custom-scan separate?**
  Yes. The three rules are stated explicitly in the
  consequences section with named cross-references. A future
  implementer cannot accidentally take a shortcut without
  contradicting the ADR.

## P3 — informational

1. **Cleanup semantics outside the scan cursor.** The ADR
   says "cleanup outside the scan cursor path" but doesn't
   pin which subsystem owns it. Likely candidates:
   - VACUUM-style background sweep per epoch retirement;
   - `ec_spire`-internal compaction triggered by epoch
     publish/retire events;
   - operator-driven via `ecaz` maintenance commands.
   Worth adding a follow-up section (or a Stage F
   implementation packet) that picks one and documents the
   contract. Optional for v1 ADR acceptance — the
   no-scan-time-writes invariant alone is enough to unblock
   the cursor wiring.

2. **Cross-reference to ADR-059.** ADR-059 owns origin-node
   heap visibility resolution. ADR-064 owns the AM-side
   row-materialization lifecycle. They're complementary —
   ADR-059 says "the origin resolves visibility before
   coordinator returns rows", ADR-064 says "the coordinator
   needs a same-relation heap row to return". Worth a
   one-line cross-reference in each ADR's "Related"
   section if it doesn't already exist.

## Status

Closed. ADR-064 pins the v1 row-materialization lifecycle
correctly: same-relation pre-existing coordinator heap
rows, epoch/MVCC-scoped, no scan-time writes, no per-cursor
proxies. The next implementation slice can build against
this contract knowing the lifecycle questions are
