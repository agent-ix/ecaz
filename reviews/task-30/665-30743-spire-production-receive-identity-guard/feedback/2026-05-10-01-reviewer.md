## Answers to the packet's review questions

- **Matching candidate-row `profile_fingerprint` bytes to
  descriptor `remote_index_identity` the right production receive
  guard before the full identity cache lands?** Yes. This is
  byte-equality against the descriptor's recorded identity at
  request-build time, which is exactly what the cache contract
  from packet 30712 specifies as the per-row check on cache hits.
  Once C3 lands the production cache reuse, this check will
  remain the per-row arm of the cache validation.
- **Should protocol/extension endpoint mismatches continue to
  collapse to `endpoint_identity_mismatch`?** Yes. The receive
  layer should report one operator-visible failure mode for
  identity divergence; finer-grained classification belongs in
  the diagnostic identity surface
  (`ec_spire_remote_search_endpoint_identity`) where operators
  inspect *why* the identity differs. Production receive's job is
  to fail closed, not classify.
- **Request-struct identity handoff sufficient for C5 to consume
  `CandidateReceiveReady` batches without re-resolving descriptor
  identity?** Yes. The identity bytes are owned on the dispatch
  and threaded into the request; C5 only needs to invoke
  `run_compact_candidate_receive` from packet 30735 and consume
  the resulting state machine without re-reading the descriptor.
