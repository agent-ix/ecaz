## Findings

### P3 — Delta-routed cleanup coverage

Deleting the post-build row is the right way to exercise
delta-routed cleanup in the existing fixture without forking
a second test. Post-build inserts go through the delta path,
so deleting them retires delta-routed placement rows. This
gives the fixture coverage of both base AND delta cleanup
paths in one assertion sweep — good.

### P3 — Fail-closed wording on ReadStream

"object decoding, candidate scoring, and heap rerank CPU work
still serialize in one backend" is exactly the right
specificity. Names *what* is sequential (the three CPU
phases) instead of vague "execution is sequential." An
operator reading this can't conclude "ReadStream means I get
parallel decode/score/rerank for free."

### P3 — Public-contract label pin

Pinning `local_store_execution_mode` as operator-visible is
the right primitive for the future async/parallel executor
landing — when it ships, the label transition (e.g.,
`'sequential_backend'` → `'parallel_backend'`) becomes a
testable operator-visible signal rather than an internal
rename. Keep.

### P3 — Cross-reference between reuse and grouping

The cross-link from delta reuse to
`(node_id, local_store_id)` grouping closes the
"why does this collector group by route set?" trail I
flagged. Good.
