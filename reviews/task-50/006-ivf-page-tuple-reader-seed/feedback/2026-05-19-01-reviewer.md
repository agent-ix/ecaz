# Approve: Slice 2 IVF page tuple reader seed

## Decision

Approve packet 006 at `6bdf5335`. This is the first real structural
lift in Task 50 — `PageTupleReader<'a>` ties the cached
`page_ptr`/`page_size`/`line_pointer_count` to the borrow of a
`LockedBufferGuard` via `PhantomData<&'a LockedBufferGuard>`. As a
direct consequence, three callable surfaces flip from `unsafe fn` to
safe `fn`:

- `visit_ivf_postings_from_buffer`
- `visit_all_ivf_postings_from_buffer`
- `visit_all_ivf_posting_refs_from_buffer`

Five caller sites no longer wrap these in `unsafe { ... }` blocks.
`src/am/ec_ivf/page.rs` drops 134 → 122 (−12, −9%). The packet
correctly identifies that the 30% top-15 target needs follow-on
mutable / WAL / posting-append rollout, and explicitly defers those.
This is the right shape for a seed.

## What's correct

- The reader's invariant is encoded *in the type system*, not in a
  SAFETY comment: the lifetime parameter forces every caller to
  hold a live `&LockedBufferGuard` for the reader's scope.
- Bounds check on `offset` against `line_pointer_count` is inside
  the reader, not at every call site — exactly the "narrow the
  unsafe block" pattern from the task doc §Techniques.
- `with_page_line_tuple_bytes` remains the *single* raw-byte
  exposure point. The reader is a wrapper, not a parallel
  implementation. Reduces audit surface.
- Mutation paths are explicitly deferred. Read-only and mutable
  visitors stay separate per the §Slice 2 design rule.
