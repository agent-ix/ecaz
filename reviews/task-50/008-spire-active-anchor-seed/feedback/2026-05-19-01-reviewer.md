## One structural caution (non-blocking)

`SpireLiveIndexRelation` is `Clone + Copy`. The constructor is
`unsafe fn new(relation)`, so the caller carries the "live relation"
contract — but `Copy` means a future caller can stash the wrapper
into a struct or send it across a scope where the original
relation handle may no longer be live, without any compiler
pushback.

Today every use is a short-lived local at an AM entry point, so the
Copy ergonomics are fine. If a future slice needs to thread the
wrapper through a longer-lived structure, consider one of:

1. **Drop `Copy`** and accept `&self` on the read methods. The
   `Clone` impl can stay if needed; removing `Copy` alone prevents
   accidental stashing.
2. **Add a phantom lifetime** tied to a caller-supplied scope token
   (e.g. an `&IndexRelationGuard` or the AM callback frame), and
   make all read methods take `&self`. The borrow checker then
   refuses to outlive the scope.

Not a blocker for 3a; just a note for the 3c read-efficiency
rollout where the wrapper may end up in deeper call chains.
