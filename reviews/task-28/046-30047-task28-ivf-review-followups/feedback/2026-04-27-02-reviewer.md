# Reviewer Follow-Up: Competitive Substrate, Not Just Correct

This addendum supplements `2026-04-27-01-reviewer.md`. The merge approval
in that file stands. This note raises the bar for what `ec_ivf` has to be
once merged, and enumerates the optimization arc that needs to follow.

## Reframing the bar

`ec_ivf` is going to carry the IVF-PQ, IVF-RaBitQ, IVF-OPQ, IVF-LVQ, and
IVF/DiskANN-hybrid lines — every paper sitting at the repo root
(`pq-ivf-jegou-1102.3828.pdf`, `ivf-rabitq-gpu-2602.23999.pdf`,
`lvq-locally-adaptive-2402.02044.pdf`) builds on IVF as substrate.
Whatever inefficiency lives in this layer compounds across every
quantizer that plugs into it, and whatever extension seams are missing
will need to be retrofitted later under load.

The bar is **competitive, not just correct**. Concretely: the merge
lands as a substrate, and a follow-up arc closes the gap before this
surface gets product-benchmarked.

## Where the gap actually is

Measured on the local DBPedia fixture (packet 30046, 100 ordered
queries, real f32 source vectors):

| corpus | recall@10 | p50 | p95 | build |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 10k × 1536, `nlists=32 nprobe=32 width=25` | 1.0000 | 178 ms | 203 ms | 24.3 s |
| 25k × 1536, same | 1.0000 | 435 ms | 456 ms | 45.0 s |

Reference points:

- **tqvector's own HNSW** at the same scale (`plan/status.md`): warm
  per-cell `p50 ≈ 14.3 ms`, cold `p50 ≈ 50 ms`. ec_ivf is **12×–30×
  slower than ec_hnsw** at comparable recall.
- **FAISS IVF / pgvector ivfflat** at this scale typically clear sub-
  50 ms p50 on commodity hardware. ec_ivf sits well above that band.
- 25k p50 is **2.4× the 10k p50** — faster than linear in candidates
  but not the sublinear curve a tuned IVF should show. That's a
  structural signal, not noise.

The "1.0000 recall" should not be misread as "this is fast." Recall is
correct; latency is the open problem.

## Concrete optimization items, grounded in code

Each item cites file:line so the coder can pick it up without a second
review pass.

### 1. Heap rerank loop has no ReadStream / prefetch — biggest lever

`src/am/ec_ivf/scan.rs:744–769` does up to `rerank_width` serial
`fetch_heap_row_version` calls per query. There is **no** ReadStream,
`PrefetchBuffer`, or `table_index_fetch_*` anywhere in `scan.rs` (grep
is empty).

`src/am/ec_ivf/page.rs:810–903` already wires PG18 ReadStream for
posting-list reads via `read_stream_begin_relation` with
`linear_prefetch_cb` / `block_sequence_prefetch_cb` — the exact pattern
that needs to apply to the rerank fetch. Heap pages for the rerank
candidates are typically scattered (one per heap_tid), so the
ReadStream prefetch callback should walk the candidate list ahead of
the scoring loop.

**Expected impact: large.** At width=25 on a cold buffer cache the
serial heap reads dominate p50.

### 2. Rerank inner product is sequential f32 by deliberate choice

`scan.rs:765–766` calls `source::negative_inner_product`, which packet
660 explicitly held to scalar f32 accumulation order to keep the SQL
`<#>` operator byte-stable for regression tests. That decision is
defensible for the SQL surface but does **not** need to apply to the
index-internal heap_f32 rerank.

**Action:** split the helper. One variant for SQL (sequential f32,
byte-stable). One for the index-internal rerank (AVX2+FMA, four-
accumulator unrolled — the kernel landed in packet 662 for HNSW
source build). Wire the IVF rerank to the latter.

**Expected impact: meaningful but not dominant** until item 1 lands;
heap fetch latency hides the IP cost on a cold cache.

### 3. Probe-candidate aggregation allocates per query

`scan.rs:685` (`best_by_heap_tid.into_values().collect`) collects probe
candidates into a `HashMap<heap_tid, EcIvfScoredCandidate>` allocated
fresh on every scan. No pooling, no bound on frontier size beyond the
natural cap from `nprobe × posting_size`.

**Action:** pool the hash table on the scan opaque, or replace with a
bounded top-`k × oversample` heap that deduplicates by heap_tid as it
inserts. The latter avoids the HashMap entirely.

**Expected impact: small in isolation, real under concurrent scan
load** — the per-query allocator pressure becomes visible at high
QPS.

### 4. Quantizer seam is declared but not wired

`src/am/ec_ivf/options.rs:33–64` defines `QuantizerProfile` listing
`turboquant | pq_fastscan | rabitq | auto`. `validate()` explicitly
rejects `PqFastScan` and `RaBitQ` at lines 63–64. `scan.rs:398, 638`
and `build.rs:493` hardcode `ProdQuantizer::cached(...)`.

The substrate has the right *shape* (versioned page format at
`page.rs:23`, `INDEX_FORMAT_VERSION = 1`; reserved option surface)
but the polymorphism is not actually implemented.

**Action:** define a trait-object or enum-dispatched scoring surface,
route `score_ip_from_parts` and the prepared-query construction
through it, let build/scan pick by reloption. Pick the dispatch shape
based on hot-path cost — enum-with-match is typically faster than
`dyn` for this kind of inner loop, but it forces every quantizer to
land in-tree.

**Frontier-research follow-on (IVF-RaBitQ, IVF-PQ-FastScan) blocks on
closing this.** This is the highest-priority *substrate* item even
though its latency impact is zero until a second quantizer ships.

### 5. Per-query state reconstruction

Each scan reconstructs `ProdQuantizer::cached(...)` and
`prepare_ip_query(...)` (`scan.rs:398–403`). `cached` *suggests* a
cache; **worth confirming** the cache is keyed by index reloid +
reloptions and survives across scans on the same index. If not, the
prepare cost is hitting every query.

### 6. Vacuum / repair surface — not read by this reviewer

`src/am/ec_ivf/vacuum.rs` (339 lines) handles dead-posting removal and
directory repair. The packet trail (`30020`–`30024`) covers the tests
but I have not read the implementation. **Worth a deeper pass before
any concurrent vacuum claims.** Vacuum performance under delete
pressure is a known IVF weak spot in the literature (FAISS doesn't
support it at all; pgvector ivfflat handles it conservatively).

### 7. Build / training surface — not read in depth

- `src/am/ec_ivf/training.rs:77` — `train_spherical_kmeans`. Deterministic
  by seed. **Confirm whether it samples from the corpus or trains on
  the full set.** Full-corpus k-means at 1M+ rows is the cliff that
  bites every IVF implementation; sampled training (e.g.,
  `min(n, 256 * nlists)`) is the standard mitigation.
- `src/am/ec_ivf/build.rs` (791 lines) — bulk assignment, populated
  pages, build stats. Not read.

### 8. Recall/latency sweep is stale relative to the post-optimization shape

The summary tested `nlists ∈ {32, 64, 128}` and `nprobe / width`
sweeps and declared `nlists=32` best on 10k/25k. Correct *given the
current scoring path*. Once items 1 and 2 land, the cost ratio
between probe-scoring and rerank shifts, and the optimal
`nlists × nprobe × width` point likely moves.

**Re-run the sweep after the optimization arc closes**, not just the
same configuration with new latency numbers. The current frontier is
a snapshot of an unoptimized cost model.

## Frontier-research seams to verify

These are the questions that matter if `ec_ivf` is going to carry the
research line:

| Seam | State | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Posting-page codec versioning | ✅ `page.rs:23` (`INDEX_FORMAT_VERSION = 1`) | None |
| Quantizer dispatch | ⚠️ Reloption present, dispatch hardcoded | Item 4 |
| Per-list metadata extensibility | ❓ Unverified | Confirm the per-list header at `page.rs:194–340` reserves bytes for RaBitQ scale factors / LVQ per-vector scales / PQ codebook ids |
| Build-time encode hook | ❓ Unverified | RaBitQ moves work into build with per-vector scales; PQ-FastScan needs codebooks. Need a hookable "encode this vector for this list" seam |
| Concurrent insert | ✅ Tested (`30016–30019`) | Per-list serialization is a real contention surface; **measure** under parallel insert before claiming the concurrency story is solved |
| Heap rerank prefetch | ❌ Missing | Item 1 |
| ReadStream on posting reads | ✅ `page.rs:810–903` | None |

## Suggested next-arc shape (post-merge)

Order by impact-per-effort, not paper-completeness.

1. **Heap-rerank ReadStream prefetch** (item 1). Biggest latency lever;
   infrastructure exists in the same module. ETA: small slice.
2. **Index-internal AVX rerank IP** (item 2). Reuse the packet-662
   kernel. ETA: small slice.
3. **Re-run recall/latency sweep** at the new cost ratio (item 8).
   Establishes the post-optimization frontier. ETA: measurement only.
4. **Wire the quantizer dispatch** (item 4). Unblocks IVF-RaBitQ and
   IVF-PQ-FastScan. ETA: medium slice. **Highest substrate priority
   even though latency impact is zero in isolation.**
5. **Pooled / bounded probe-candidate aggregation** (item 3).
   Concurrent-scan win. ETA: small slice.
6. **Deeper read of `build.rs`, `training.rs`, `vacuum.rs`** (items 6,
   7). Surfaces whatever is hiding before the next product gate.
7. **First quantizer-extension paper landing** — pick one of
   IVF-RaBitQ or IVF-PQ-FastScan and drive it end-to-end on the new
   substrate. Forces the seam from item 4 to be honest.

After items 1–3, ec_ivf should be in the **sub-50 ms p50 band on the
10k slice at recall 0.99+**. After item 4, the substrate is ready for
the frontier-research arc. Anything short of item 1+2+3 closing
should not be quoted as ec_ivf's performance ceiling.

## Disposition

Stays **approve for merge**, with the explicit understanding that:

- The merge ships ec_ivf as a **substrate**, not as a product.
- A tracked Task 28 follow-on lane is opened immediately, committing
  to items 1–7 above.
- The next product benchmark of ec_ivf does not happen until at least
  items 1, 2, and 8 have landed. Without that, this branch's recall
  numbers will be quoted out of context as ec_ivf's performance
  ceiling.

The merge itself is ready. The work to make `ec_ivf` a competitive
substrate for frontier research is enumerated above and should be
carried as the next Task 28 lane.
