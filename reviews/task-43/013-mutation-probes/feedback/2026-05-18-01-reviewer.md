  total. Matches the mutation-test sanity gate from the closeout
  review of packet 005.
- **Every failing log records `test result: FAILED` and
  `COMMAND_EXIT_CODE=101`.** That is the exact behavior the task
  spec asked for ("fails predictably if an obvious bug is injected").
- **Source state after probes is clean.**
  `source-status-after-probes.log` records zero changes under `src`,
  `hardening`, `docs`; `git-diff-check.log` exits 0. The mutations
  are temporary by construction.
- **Patches are durable evidence.** Saved as packet-local
  `artifacts/patches/*.diff` so a future reviewer can replay any
  single probe deterministically without re-deriving the diff.

## Findings

- **One probe per subsystem is the minimum bar, not depth.** The
  campaign-tracker rules call this G7 done; a deeper future cycle
  could probe per-test (e.g. every named test in packet 008's 32-test
  promotion). Not required for this campaign; flagging only.
- **Probes target one obvious-bug shape per subsystem.** They prove
  Miri/test would catch *that* class of regression; subtler bugs
  (e.g. a borrow split that preserves the algorithmic answer) are
  not directly probed. Acceptable — this is sensitivity, not
  exhaustive testing — but worth knowing what mutation probes do and
  don't prove.
