# Review: Task 43 Coverage Survey And Strategy (packet 001)

## Summary

Planning-only packet. Inventories the existing 19 `miri_` tests across 7
source files, names the in-scope surfaces from
`plan/tasks/43-miri-careful-depth.md`, and proposes a 6-checkpoint
sequence: lane infrastructure → pure graph → candidate merge →
serialization/vacuum → remote payload → many-seeds.

## Confirmations

- **Subsystem mapping is complete.** Every surface called out in
  `plan/tasks/43-miri-careful-depth.md` (SPIRE coordinator, DiskANN
  graph, HNSW graph, top-k merge, vacuum, remote parser, serialization)
  is named with concrete file paths and pure-helper candidates.
- **Promotion-over-creation policy is explicit.** Strategy commits to
  adding `miri_` prefixes to existing bounded tests rather than copying
  test bodies, which is the right call for keeping the lane small.
- **The hardest blocker is named up front.** "There are no existing
  `miri_` tests that spawn threads" is in the strategy as a hard
  constraint on what many-seeds can claim. Good honesty in the plan.
- **The pgrx-tangle escape hatch is named.** For SPIRE coordinator and
  remote parser, the strategy says "extract a small pure helper first"
  rather than promising to drag pgrx into Miri.

## Findings

- **Many-seeds strategy is a deferral.** The strategy says
  `miri-many-seeds` should "exist as a lane" but explicitly tells the
  reader that until a threaded `miri_` test exists, the lane just
  reruns non-threaded tests. This is the right call for an
  infrastructure-first cycle, but it bakes a residual risk that the
  closeout must own. See review of packet 005.
- **No exit gate for "broad enough" coverage.** The strategy lists six
  checkpoints but does not state a numeric or percentage floor for how
  many `miri_` tests per subsystem qualifies as "covered". A reader
  can't tell from this packet whether one `miri_` test per subsystem
  is enough or whether the spec demands more. Closeout packet 005
  partially addresses this with two tests per major subsystem.
- **No triage plan for Stacked Borrows vs Tree Borrows differences.**
  The task spec asked the inaugural packet to triage differences; the
  strategy doesn't pre-commit to where that triage lives or what to do
  on disagreement. (As it happens, SB and TB both passed cleanly, so
  there is nothing to triage — but that's not knowable in advance.)

## Verdict

Approve as planning. Scope identification is complete, blockers are
flagged honestly, and the promotion-over-creation policy is sound for
a depth-expansion lane. The narrowness of the many-seeds claim and the
absence of a per-subsystem coverage floor are concerns that
follow-through packets must answer.
