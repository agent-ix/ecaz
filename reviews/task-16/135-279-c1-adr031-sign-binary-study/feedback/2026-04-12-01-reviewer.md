# Review: Packet 279 — C1 ADR-031 Sign-Derived Binary Prefilter Study

Reviewer: agent-ix (via code-review)
Date: 2026-04-12

## Assessment: Strong study — correct conclusions, one important nuance to flag

### What landed

Extended `approx_score_study` with a `binary-sign` study mode. Derived binary
codes from existing 4-bit centroid signs. Measured rank correlation, top-k
capture, and per-score microbenchmarks on the real 10K corpus.

### On the results

The headline numbers are clear:

- `spearman_rho=0.932` — strong enough for a conservative survivor filter
- `cached_binary: 9.8ns` vs `exact: 1436.9ns` — 146x speedup when cached
- `derived_binary: 5820ns` — 4x slower than exact, completely unusable on-the-fly

The correct call was made: cached/stored binary codes only, no on-the-fly
derivation.

### The nuance worth flagging

The study correctly notes that binary scoring is "not strong enough to use
aggressively at small survivor budgets" — top-50 still misses some exact top-10.
Packet 281 later confirms this empirically: the `drop=0/2/4` budget is
effectively flat, meaning the binary rejection step itself contributes almost
nothing to the latency win. The real win was making exact scoring lazy.

This means the binary prefilter's value is more architectural (gating exact
scorer invocations) than scoring-quality-driven. The filter quality is "good
enough not to hurt" rather than "good enough to aggressively prune." That's
fine for the current shape, but it means investing in better binary code
quality (e.g., full RaBitQ rather than sign-derived) would likely not improve
latency — the bottleneck is elsewhere.

### On the study methodology

Using the offline `approx_score_study` binary before touching scan runtime is
the right sequencing. It prevents wasting integration effort on a dead-end and
gives clean reproducible numbers. Well structured.

### Verdict

Strong study packet. Correctly identified the viable integration path (cached
only) and the non-viable one (on-the-fly derivation). The survivor-budget
observation is the most important finding for future work.
