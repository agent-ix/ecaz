# Review: Task 41 shared index scan guard (31198)

## Summary

Promotes `IndexScanGuard` into `src/storage/scan_guard.rs`,
deletes the planner-local copy (31175) and the
`DebugIndexScanGuard` in scan_debug.rs (31182). Migrates both
callers. Closes my 31195 finding #1. Baseline 4,237 → 4,235
(−2).

This packet's interesting bit isn't the consolidation itself —
it's the **constructor signature**:

```rust
pub(crate) fn begin(
    heap_relation: &HeapRelationGuard,
    index_relation: &IndexRelationGuard,
    snapshot: &ActiveSnapshotGuard,
    nkeys: i32,
    norderbys: i32,
) -> Option<Self>
```

Taking typed `&Guard` references — rather than raw
`pg_sys::Relation` / `pg_sys::Snapshot` pointers — is the **first
compiler-enforced lifetime relationship between RAII wrappers in
the codebase**. Callers cannot construct an `IndexScanGuard`
without holding live guards for all three preconditions. The
borrow checker now prevents the "raw pointer outlives the
owning guard" failure mode at the guard-composition boundary.
This is the type-enforced safety upgrade I noted as missing
across the relation-guard surface in earlier feedback.

## Confirmations

- **PG18 + pre-PG18 signatures preserved.** The `#[cfg(feature
  = "pg18")]` / `#[cfg(not(feature = "pg18"))]` split inside

  alternative (raw pointers) would have forced every caller to

  `norderbys = 0`, followed by `index_rescan` and
  `index_getnext_slot` — verified by inspecting the planner
  callsite diff.
- **Baseline arithmetic.** −2 (two module-local impl bodies
  collapsed into one shared body).

## Findings

### 1. Convention milestone worth noting

`IndexScanGuard::begin`'s typed-borrow constructor signature is
the precedent for future guard families that depend on others.
The buffer/page/WAL track's eventual `BufferReadGuard` will
likely take `&IndexRelationGuard` or `&HeapRelationGuard` the
same way; `LwLockGuard::acquire` will take `&IndexRelationGuard`.
Worth a one-line addition to the strategy matrix:

> Guard constructors that depend on other resources should take
> typed `&Guard` references rather than raw pointers, so the
> borrow checker enforces lifetime invariants at composition
> boundaries.

### 2. Naming consistency note

Now five resource families share names across the codebase:
`IndexRelationGuard`/`HeapRelationGuard`/`RelationGuard`,
`ActiveSnapshotGuard`, `TupleTableSlotGuard`, `IndexScanGuard`.
Convention so far has been `{Noun}Guard` (or `{Verb}{Noun}Guard`
for `ActiveSnapshotGuard`). When `BufferReadGuard` /
`BufferWriteGuard` / `LwLockGuard` land, sticking to that pattern
will keep the surface readable.
