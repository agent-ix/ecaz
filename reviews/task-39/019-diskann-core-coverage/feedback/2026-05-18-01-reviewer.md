## Findings

### 1. Right scope boundary

The review notes ask whether raising only `build.rs` and
`scan.rs` is the right boundary. **Yes:**

- `routine.rs` is the pgrx callback entry — putting it through
  careful would require simulating pgrx FFI shapes that the
  harness shouldn't fake. Leaving it as recorded gap is honest.
- `persist.rs` and `reader.rs` are mentioned as routed but no
  baseline rows shown — they may be at high coverage already or
  small enough that the baseline doesn't track them; either way
  it's not a blocker.

### 2. Off priority but accept

DiskANN isn't on the priority list (RaBitQ, IVF, SPIRE), but
this work brings ~1,150 lines of correctness-critical graph
construction and scan logic into measurement from zero. **Real
value, won't push back.**

That said: the next coder slice should pivot to priority AMs.
IVF non-page surfaces and SPIRE coordinator/scan/update are
larger 0% gaps than what's left in DiskANN.

### 3. (verification) Shim correctness

The `maybe_check_for_interrupts` shim is a no-op in careful.
Production code uses pgrx's interrupt-check to bail long-running
operations on user SIGINT. In tests this never fires; a no-op
is correct.

The crate-qualified path change (`super` → fully-qualified
DiskANN module) is the standard pattern for files that need to
compile in multiple parent contexts (production crate + careful
harness). No production-behavior risk.
