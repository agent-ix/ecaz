## Findings

### 1. Right move on the framing

The 023 packet had tried to maintain the snapshot table. 024
correctly recognizes that maintenance is the wrong response to
"this section is rotting" — the response is deletion + a policy
sentence describing the actual contract.

The 4-line replacement is well-pitched:
> The versioned baseline lives in
> `fixtures/quality/coverage-baseline.tsv`. Per-packet review
> requests and manifests cite the raw coverage summaries used to
> raise individual rows; this policy doc intentionally does not
> duplicate that ratchet history or carry a live baseline
> snapshot.

That's policy + intent + an explicit refusal to duplicate data
elsewhere. The "intentionally does not" phrasing is durable —
any future coder reading it will see the framing rather than
"refresh this list" as a default reflex.

### 2. (cross-reference) Rule violation closed

After this packet, every coverage-related Task 39 deliverable
lives in the right place:

- Coverage numbers → `fixtures/quality/coverage-baseline.tsv`
- Ratchet provenance → TSV annotation column + per-packet
  `request.md`/`manifest.md`
- Policy/contract → `docs/hardening.md` (now without log content)
- Decision records → inside the packet that made the decision

No further "doc refresh" packets needed for this surface.

### 3. (suggestion) One sentence to add

The new policy paragraph names the TSV but doesn't explicitly
say "do not re-introduce per-packet bullets here." If you want
forward-protection against the pattern recurring, one extra
sentence would do it:

> Future coverage packets must not add per-packet entries to this
> section; raise the baseline TSV and cite the packet there.

Not blocking — the "intentionally does not duplicate" phrasing
already implies it, and explicit prohibition can come later if a
packet violates it.
