## Findings

### 1. Scaffold-first approach is right

The packet's design is intentionally minimal: stub enough `pg_sys`
surface that `page.rs` *compiles* in the careful crate, then add
tests that exercise only the early-error paths that return `Err`
before any real page operation. That's exactly the right
incremental scaffold — proves the routing works without requiring
the full page emulator up front.

11.01% from zero is also the right framing — six tests, six early
returns, ~11% of `page.rs` is short-circuit error paths. Honest
number, not a vanity gain.

### 2. The backing-page emulator is the load-bearing next slice

The packet's "follow-ups" section names it precisely:
> The remaining 89% of `page.rs` lines need a backing-page
> emulator inside the pg_sys mocks (real `PageInit` /
> `PageAddItemExtended` semantics with a per-buffer 8K backing
> buffer and ItemId table). That's a multi-hour slice in itself.

That's the right scope of the follow-up. Without the emulator,
the encode/decode success paths can't run; the coverage stays
at ~11%. With it, page.rs likely climbs to 80-95%.

**Suggested split for the emulator slice:**
- Phase 1: 8K backing buffer + ItemId table + `PageInit` /
  `PageAddItemExtended` / `PageGetItem` / `PageGetItemId` /
  `PageGetMaxOffsetNumber` working against the buffer.
- Phase 2: `PageGetFreeSpace` / `PageIndexTupleDeleteNoCompact`
  semantics.
- Phase 3: `GenericXLog` state-machine consistency (Register →
  Finish writes the buffer; Abort discards).

Each phase is testable independently. Phase 1 alone probably
gets page.rs from 11% → 60%+.

### 3. Same infrastructure unblocks relation_store.rs

The packet correctly observes that `ec_spire/storage/relation_store.rs`
(the 4th 0% row in the baseline) shares the
`LockedBufferGuard` + page-add flow. Once the emulator lands,
that file becomes tractable too.

Net effect of the emulator slice: closes 2 of the 4 remaining
0% rows in the baseline (`page.rs` + `relation_store.rs`).
That's the highest-leverage 0%-row work remaining.
