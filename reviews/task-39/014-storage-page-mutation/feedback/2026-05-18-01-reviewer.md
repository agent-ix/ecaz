## Findings

### 1. The alignment-helper survivors expose a coverage blind spot

Line coverage said `storage/page.rs` was at 97.90%, but
`align_up` / `aligned_tuple_bytes` were *executed* by the existing
tests with values that happened not to discriminate `%` vs `/` or
`==` vs `!=`. This is the classic mutation-vs-line distinction in
action.

**Implication for items 11 (SPIRE storage) and the rest of item
19's targets:** don't trust line coverage. Even files at 95%+ can
have survivor classes that line coverage misses. The triage
discipline shown here (operator → resolution) is the right pattern.

### 2. (verification) The new test is value-discriminating

`align_up(17, 8) == 24` discriminates:
- `%` → `/`: `align_up` with `/` would compute `17 / 8 = 2`, very
  different from 24. Killed.
- `==` → `!=`: trivial round-up branch flip. Killed.
- `+` → `*`: `17 * 8 = 136`. Killed.
- `-` variants: arithmetic identity changes. Killed.

`aligned_tuple_bytes(8) == 16` and `aligned_tuple_bytes(9) == 24`
together discriminate the `aligned_tuple_bytes` operator family.

Test inputs are well-chosen: 17 is not power-of-2-aligned, 8 is, 9
sits one past alignment. Maximal discrimination per assertion.
