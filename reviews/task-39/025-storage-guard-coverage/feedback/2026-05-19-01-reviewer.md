# Review: Task 39 storage guard coverage (025)

## Summary

Closes one of the highest-leverage 0% gaps in the baseline: the
seven `src/storage/*_guard.rs` RAII wrappers (Task 41's
deliverables) are now measured. **All seven from 0% to
82-100%**, with 10 guard tests verifying constructor/drop
ownership semantics for buffers, locks, relations, scans, slots,

The interesting move: rather than depending on a real pgrx
backend, this packet ships a **582-line `hardening/careful/src/pg_guards.rs`
fake `pg_sys` surface** that the production guard files compile
against. Counter-based `AtomicUsize` "did this FFI get called?"
checks let the tests assert RAII ownership without a live
backend.

This is the right shape — RAII semantics (acquire on construct,
release on drop) is observable through call counts; that doesn't
require real Postgres state.

## Findings

### 1. The fake `pg_sys` is reusable infrastructure

This packet adds 582 lines of fake `pg_sys` covering: Buffer,
BlockNumber, LOCKMODE, Oid, Page, Snapshot, ScanData, LWLock,
SnapshotData, RelationData, TupleTableSlot, SPITupleTable,
plus the 15 atomic counters tracking FFI call observability.

This isn't single-use scaffolding — it's the foundation for
testing any future RAII or pgrx-bound code in the careful lane.
A few use cases that this unlocks:

- **GenericXLog transactions** (`src/storage/wal.rs`'s
  `GenericXLogTxn`): same RAII pattern, currently uncovered.
  The fake `pg_sys` doesn't have the `GenericXLog*` symbols yet
  — would need an extension to the fake.
- **More AM pgrx-bound files**: instead of cfg-gating the
  callback glue out of careful (packet 015's pattern), some
  callback files could potentially use the counter-based
  approach to verify cleanup semantics.

Not blocking — just noting the reusability. The fake should be
treated as a shared resource going forward, not duplicated.

### 2. (verification) RAII semantics observable through counters

The pattern is: each guard's `Drop` impl calls a `pg_sys::*_close`
or `*_release` function. The fake increments a counter; the
test asserts the count after the guard goes out of scope. For
RAII the only thing that matters is "the FFI got called exactly
once on drop, never on construct, never twice." That's exactly
what counters capture.

Stronger than coverage-only: this **proves the RAII contract**
rather than just exercising the lines. If a future refactor
accidentally calls `index_close` twice or leaks the buffer, the
counter assertion catches it. Real correctness value, not
number-moving.

### 3. Three files below 100% — natural residuals

- **`scan_guard.rs` at 95.83%** — the remaining ~4% is probably
  the error-path Drop arm (when `IndexScan` failed to allocate
  and the guard wraps a null). Worth a follow-up assertion if
  the gap matters.
- **`slot_guard.rs` at 91.30%** — similar; probably the
  error-path or the `TupleDesc` ref-count arm.
- **`snapshot_guard.rs` at 82.93%** — largest gap; likely the
  ActiveSnapshot push/pop sequencing or the registered-snapshot
  vs. active-snapshot discriminant.

None are blocking; all are above the 80% floor. Worth one
follow-up to pin which lines remain unreached and decide if
they're testable in careful or need to stay as recorded
residual.

### 4. (cross-reference) Closes an explicit Task 39 gap

The original Task 39 plan named `src/storage/*_guard.rs` as part
of the storage codec scope (implicit — storage/page.rs was
named, the guards landed later via Task 41). At 0% they were a
visible hole in the baseline; this packet closes it.

This + 019 (DiskANN core build/scan) + 021 (SPIRE storage) +
022 (SPIRE relation_plan) means most of the remaining
"recorded gap" surface from a few iterations ago has shifted
into measurement. What's still at 0%: `ec_spire/page.rs`,
`relation_store.rs`, `coordinator/**`, AM callback files
(routine.rs for each AM).

### 5. (suggestion) Consider extending the fake for GenericXLog

The fake `pg_sys` is one extension away from also testing
`src/storage/wal.rs`'s `GenericXLogTxn`. Adding
`GenericXLogStart`/`GenericXLogFinish`/`GenericXLogAbort`
counters would let a future packet cover the WAL RAII contract
the same way this packet covers the others.

Not in scope for this packet. Worth a one-line note in the
manifest for the next storage-guard-adjacent slice.
