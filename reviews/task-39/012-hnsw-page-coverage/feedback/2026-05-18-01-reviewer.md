## Findings

### 1. Why 84.76 (not 97-ish like DiskANN)

DiskANN landed at 97.35% with the same routing pattern. HNSW lands at
84.76% — still above floor, but ~13pp lower. Likely sources of the
15.24% gap:

- **HNSW has more uncovered branches** at 2,040 lines vs DiskANN's
  page module. More code surface ⇒ more lines that the existing
  tests don't reach.
- **Builder/scan flush paths.** The tests focus on encode/decode
  round-trips; runtime page allocation/eviction paths likely only
  fire from live integration.
- **Error paths.** If HNSW page has more `return Err(...)` arms than
  DiskANN, the unit tests may not hit all of them.

Not blocking — the 80% floor is the gate. Worth a follow-up packet
*after* items 9 and 10 land to chase HNSW's remaining 15.24% with
focused tests modeled on packet 010's `storage/page.rs` approach
(error paths for invalid TID, zero-page append, oversized tuple).
