### 1. The closeout argument is correct

Per the task's own framing:
> A baseline coverage % per critical module is recorded in
> docs/hardening.md so future regressions are visible.

The exit criterion is **recording the baseline**, not pushing
every file above 80%. The 80% target in `docs/hardening.md` is
explicitly "after burn-in" guidance, not an exit gate. The three
files still below floor or at 0% (`relation_store.rs` 58.10%,
`coordinator/diagnostics.rs` 0%, `ec_diskann/routine.rs` 0%) are
recorded baselines — regressions on them will be visible.

My earlier insistence on 80% on every file was scope-padding
against my own 003 reviewer-authored coverage plan, not the
actual task close criterion. The closeout correctly defers to
the task plan as written.

### 2. Three recorded-baseline gaps are accurately characterized

The closeout names each with its specific blocker:

- **`relation_store.rs` at 58.10%**: remaining ~22% is
  crafted-byte error branches + PG18-only `read_stream` loop
  (emulator stubs). Phase-2 emulator slice would close it.

  into `ec_spire/mod.rs`, pulls coordinator/types.rs (1833
  lines), `quantizer::*`, Spi surface, and four accumulator
  types. Multi-packet careful-side scaffold OR live pgrx
  coverage.
- **`ec_diskann/routine.rs` at 0%**: 4291 lines, heavy `pgrx::*`
  + `extern "C-unwind"`. Substantial new pgrx-bindings shadow
  types needed.

All three blockers are real (verified pattern from 033/034/035
when the emulator was built — each pgrx surface added required
the matching shim work). None are urgent given the recorded
baselines.
