## Findings

### 1. The careful harness boundary is right

Review notes ask: "Whether the careful harness boundary is
appropriate: it covers pure Spire storage codecs and in-memory
local stores, but deliberately does not claim relation-backed
PostgreSQL storage behavior."

**Yes, boundary is right:**
- The 11 files routed are pure data structures + codecs (header
  decoding, leaf encoding, routing-delta math, vec_id
  manipulation, in-memory local stores). All testable without a
  live PostgreSQL relation.
- `relation_store.rs` (still 0%) is the PG-relation I/O layer —
  reading/writing buffers, taking locks, advancing snapshots.
  That can't run in careful without faking pgrx; correctly left
  out.
- `relation_plan.rs` is split off into 022 because it had one PG
  identifier-limit dependency that needed a small helper to
  isolate. Right call to keep it separate.

### 2. Files below 80% — natural gaps, worth follow-up

Three files landed below the 80% floor:

- **`local_store_set.rs` at 41.52%.** The lowest. Likely because
  the file's primary path is multi-store routing that exercises
  through callbacks not yet in careful. Worth a follow-up packet
  to pin which lines aren't reached; may be unreachable in
  pure-Rust paths or may need targeted tests.
- **`vec_id.rs` at 69.05%.** Almost certainly missing the
  PG-specific encoding paths (`pg_sys::ItemPointer` ↔ vec_id).
  Pure-Rust round-trips may be sufficient if those paths exist
  separately.
- **`leaf_v2.rs` at 71.76%.** Largest file likely to have
  PG-bound paths (buffer-format conversions). Marginal climb
  possible without crossing into PG-bound code.

**Not blocking** this packet — but a follow-up "SPIRE storage
floor compliance" packet that pushes these three above 80% would
close the priority cleanly. Or accept them as below-floor
recorded baselines and move to the next priority gap.

### 3. Mutation campaign should follow

Coverage gains alone don't probe correctness depth. The SPIRE
storage codecs are correctness-critical (leaf encoding governs
the on-disk format; assignment governs which leaf a vec lands
in). **A mutation campaign on at least the top-3 files
(`leaf_v1.rs`, `leaf_v2.rs`, `assignment.rs`) is the natural
next slice** before declaring SPIRE storage done.

Same pattern as packets 014/015/016/017/018: coverage first,
mutation second.
